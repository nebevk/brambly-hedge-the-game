## 1. What this plan is for

Marketing for this game is not a launch-month activity; it is a **30-month drumbeat with five hard gates**, and the drumbeat is fed by one endlessly repeatable visual moment. The cozy market is glutted (~375 cozy-tagged Steam releases in 2024, median 2025 Steam release revenue ~$249 — [market analysis §2](../research/08-market-analysis.md)), and the survivors share three traits: a one-GIF hook, distinctive art, and ≥90% positive reviews. This plan exists to make sure we ship all three and never waste a one-shot beat (the announce, the single Next Fest, the launch window).

## 2. Naming discipline & the licensor dependency

Everything in this plan runs under the public codename **Project Hedgerow** until a licence is signed ([D1](../gdd/00-decisions.md)). Operational consequences:

- **Every public marketing asset is built rename-safe.** No canon character or place names in trailers, capsules, clip captions, page text, or file metadata. Canon-named material stays in the private canon layer. This is not extra work if the rename-safe data layer (D1) is respected — marketing assets simply pull from the same layer.
- **The June 2027 announce is planned as a codename announce.** The Rockpool pitch targets ~Q2 2027 with a hard 3-month decision window ([D1](../gdd/00-decisions.md)); the announce trailer locks ~6 weeks before Wholesome Direct. The licence therefore cannot be assumed for the announce. If it signs early, we announce under the licensed name; if it signs after, **"Project Hedgerow is Brambly Hedge: The Game" becomes a reserve second beat** — a free news cycle we bank, not a schedule risk.
- **Before any licence is signed, confirm in writing:** (a) streaming/UGC/content-creation rights for players and creators; (b) marketing-asset approval turnaround. An approval loop longer than ~5 working days kills the weekly clip cadence this entire plan runs on ([market analysis — implications](../research/08-market-analysis.md)). This is a contract red line, alongside the publisher terms floor in [funding.md](./funding.md).
- Kickstarter, if ever used, additionally needs explicit licensor consent to crowdfund ([funding.md §7](./funding.md)).

## 3. The one-GIF hook doctrine

**The hook is the dollhouse zoom.** One continuous 6–10 second shot: Vista band over the hedgerow at diorama tilt-shift → zoom through the canopy → a 45° yaw snap → the roof dither-fades away into a fully furnished mouse kitchen mid-supper. That is the whole pitch with zero words, and no comparable title owns it ([synthesis §1.2](../research/00-synthesis.md), [camera spec D3](../gdd/00-decisions.md)).

Rules, testable:

1. **Every published asset serves the hook or a named variant of it.** Before posting, ask: does this clip show *scale magic* (big world → tiny inhabited room) or *community-in-motion* (NPCs visibly working/celebrating together)? If neither, it does not go out.
2. **Maintain a variant library** so the cadence never starves: season flip over the identical framing ("same hedge, four books", [D4](../gdd/00-decisions.md)); community-project time-lapse (bridge mended across in-game days); NPC work shifts; the weekly supper filling up; Near-zoom "pallet moments" (light through stems); photo-mode storybook plates.
3. **The announce trailer opens on the hook** — within the first 5 seconds, no logo card first. Second hook, stated in text on the page not the trailer: *"You don't build your empire. You build the community."*
4. **Gate G1 (M2) is the hook's truth test** ([D16](../gdd/00-decisions.md)): 3–5 GIFs posted under the codename; if none earns meaningful organic traction, the art direction iterates before anything else is built. Tiny Glade rode exactly this loop from WIP clips to 1.37M wishlists with zero paid marketing ([market analysis §3](../research/08-market-analysis.md)).
5. The **photo mode with one-click "storybook plate" render ships in the first public build** ([D13](../gdd/00-decisions.md)) — it turns every demo player and creator into a supplier of hook-shaped assets.

## 4. Wishlist gates and funnel maths

| Milestone | Wishlists | Timing | If missed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-demo floor | ≥1,000 | before public demo release | delay demo, keep cadence |
| **Next Fest entry floor (G3)** | **≥2,000 (10k+ ideal)** | before the single Next Fest | delay the fest — we get exactly one |
| Popular Upcoming visibility | ~7,000 | any time pre-launch | milestone, not a gate |
| G4 go/no-go trajectory | 7–10k trajectory | M12 (with funding alternative, [D16](../gdd/00-decisions.md)) | pivot to fallback scope |
| **Launch floor** | **25,000** | launch decision, ~6 weeks pre-launch | slip one quarter ([D11](../gdd/00-decisions.md)) |
| Launch goal / stretch | 50,000 / 100,000 | — | — |

Planning maths (median cases, not Tiny Glade outliers): wishlist→week-1 conversion is ~0.10–0.15× for games priced above $10; 50k wishlists ≈ 5,000–7,500 week-1 units ≈ $85–130k gross ([market analysis §5](../research/08-market-analysis.md)). Budget against the median; treat anything better as runway, not plan.

Dates assume Plan A month 1 = July 2026 and the Plan B launch window of Q3 2028 ([D16](../gdd/00-decisions.md), [roadmap](./roadmap.md)).

| **June 2027** | **The single Steam Next Fest** (fallback: October 2027 if G3's ≥2k floor is missed) | Enter with ≥2k WL (ideally 10k+, [D16](../gdd/00-decisions.md)); demo to cozy streamers/curators 2–4 weeks prior; **$5–10k micro-influencer support** live during the fest (§9). | May–Jun (annually) | Cozy & Family Friendly Games Celebration | Recurring Steam event with large curator/newsletter following |

| **Sep–Oct 2028** | **LAUNCH** — only if ≥25k wishlists | −10% launch discount; Sept–Oct is proven cozy season (Tiny Glade, Webfishing) and dodges the late-Oct/Nov AAA wall. **If <25k WL: slip one quarter** (to Dec 2028, per [roadmap Phase B3](./roadmap.md) — never into November) and keep the cadence running |
| Launch +6–12 mo | Switch 2 port (post-recoup, §12); free seasonal update (festivals #3/#4 per [D10](../gdd/00-decisions.md)) timed to a Wholesome sale | Year-2 revenue tail |

**The fest slot is fixed by the brief:** [D16](../gdd/00-decisions.md) places the single Next Fest at M10–12 — June 2027, entered only with the G3 floor of ≥2,000 wishlists banked ([D11](../gdd/00-decisions.md)); if the floor is missed, the entry defers to October 2027 ([roadmap §2/§4](./roadmap.md)). It cannot sit later: G4's entry criteria at M12 require Next Fest data (or the deferral decision) in hand. The governing rule is "one fest, ever, never entered under 2k wishlists" ([scoping research §4](../research/13-scoping-and-production.md)) — any move of the slot would be an owner-level edit to [D16](../gdd/00-decisions.md), not a call this plan can make.

**Reconciling page-live with the ≤18-month rule:** underperformers average 411 days page-to-launch vs 214 for overperformers, but Zukowski's counterweight is that a viral moment without a live page wastes thousands of wishlists ([market analysis §5](../research/08-market-analysis.md)). So the quiet page goes live M4–5 and carries **no beats**; the ≤18-month staleness window ([D11](../gdd/00-decisions.md)) is enforced on the *announced* page: June 2027 announce → Sept–Oct 2028 launch = 15–16 months.

## 6. The weekly clip cadence (named owner required)

- **Cadence: 1–2 clips/week, every week, from M5 (page live) to launch.** ~44 weeks/year × 2 years ≈ 130–200 clips. The variant library (§3) exists so this never becomes "what do we post".
- **A single named owner** is accountable for the cadence — captured, cut, captioned, posted, replied-to. Assign by name at the M5 page-live kickoff and record it in [roadmap.md](./roadmap.md). Default split for a 2-person team: the art-side developer captures and cuts (2–4 h/week, batch-captured monthly), either partner posts and replies. If the team is solo, the floor is 1 clip/week — the cadence never pauses, it only thins.
- **Channels, in priority order:** TikTok + Instagram Reels (paid-capable, female-majority cozy audience — Hozy's playbook), YouTube Shorts, Reddit (r/CozyGamers and adjacent — Tiny Glade's spike engine), X/Bluesky for the gamedev/press graph. Same clip everywhere; caption tuned per channel.
- **Format:** 6–30 s, vertical-first master with a 16:9 crop, no text needed to land the hook, loopable. Every clip ends on a composed "book plate" frame.
- Monthly devlog (blog/Steam news post) once the page is live — long-form trust-building behind the short-form hook ([synthesis §4, risk 10](../research/00-synthesis.md)).

## 7. Steam page optimisation

- **Tags (order matters for discovery):** Life Sim, Exploration, Cozy, Cute, Relaxing, Village Sim; add Farming Sim only if gardening ships at 1.0 ([D5](../gdd/00-decisions.md) Should). Mechanics-forward framing — never "cozy" alone; farming/life-sim is the 2nd-healthiest genre on Steam (8.3% hit rate) while vibe-only cozy underperforms ([market analysis §2](../research/08-market-analysis.md)).
- **Capsule:** the dollhouse cutaway, readable at small-capsule size (231×87 px) — one warm lit interior against the dark hedge exterior. Test at thumbnail size before approving; if the cutaway doesn't read, the capsule fails.
- **Short description:** verbs first, then the community hook. Template: *"Gather, cook and lend a paw in a hedgerow village of mice. Zoom from the whole hedge into every furnished burrow. You don't build your empire — you build the community."* (Rename-safe; canon-named variant prepared but unpublished.)
- **Screenshots:** 6–8, each individually passing the book-plate test ([D3](../gdd/00-decisions.md)); the set must include ≥1 dollhouse interior, 1 Vista diorama, 1 Near pallet-moment, 1 communal scene (supper or project work), and ≥1 per shipped season once seasons exist.
- **Localised page text in all 7 launch languages (EFIGS + Simplified Chinese + Japanese) from the June 2027 announce.** Page text is a few hundred words — trivial cost, outsized reach: English + Simplified Chinese alone cover ~63% of Steam users, and JA is IP-strategic ([D15](../gdd/00-decisions.md), [market analysis §9](../research/08-market-analysis.md)).
- **Accessibility metadata filled completely** (scalable text, remapping, no fail states, reduced-motion camera, colour-blind-safe highlighting — all real features per [D15](../gdd/00-decisions.md)). Steam's accessibility fields are now a filterable discovery surface ([market analysis §10](../research/08-market-analysis.md)).
- Demo page linked from the main page during the fest window; trailer refreshed at announce, fest, and launch — never older than 6 months once announced.

## 8. Demo design

The demo is a marketing artifact with telemetry, not a slice of convenience ([scoping research §4](../research/13-scoping-and-production.md)).

- **Target: 30–45 minute median playtime** ([D11](../gdd/00-decisions.md)). Benchmark tiers: Gold = 38 min median ≈ ~1,200 wishlists earned; Diamond = 65 min ≈ ~7,000. We tune for solid Gold and let Diamond happen.
- **Contents:** summer only; one full in-game day (~20 real minutes, [D6](../gdd/00-decisions.md) clock); a compressed community-project arc (the footbridge repair — proposal seconded, one contribution, NPCs visibly at work, completion witnessed); 3 fully scheduled NPCs + 2 presence NPCs (5 on screen, per [GDD 20 Step 3](../gdd/20-mvp.md)); journal v1; gather→cook→share loop; photo mode with storybook-plate export; controller + KB/M; full options/accessibility menu.
- **The golden moment:** the demo ends on the shared hedgerow supper ([D9](../gdd/00-decisions.md)) — the capstone that trailers, creators, and the wishlist prompt all point at.
- **Cut from the demo:** gardening, fishing, festivals, VO, other seasons ([D16 demo cut lines via roadmap](./roadmap.md)).
- **Instrumented telemetry from day one:** median playtime, drop-off points, completion rate, demo→wishlist conversion. This data is the publisher pitch deck's spine and the pre-condition for signing anything ([D16](../gdd/00-decisions.md), [funding.md §6](./funding.md)).
- Demo to cozy streamers/curators with a key-mail 2–4 weeks before the fest; demo quality bar = launch quality bar, because **demo bugs are review events** (§10).

## 9. Content-creator strategy (cozy TikTok / YouTube)

- **Organic first.** A Little to the Left's 65M-view TikTok cascade was organic — seeded by a good demo and key-mailing, not spend ([market analysis §6](../research/08-market-analysis.md)). Build a curated key list of 100–200 cozy creators (Twitch/YouTube/TikTok — the MadMorph/LuxieGames tier and below) from M6 onward; mail keys at demo, fest, and launch.
- **Creator kit:** press kit (presskit.gg pattern), pre-cut b-roll of the hook and its variants, GIF pack, logo/key art, and the photo mode — creators who can one-click a storybook plate will post storybook plates.
- **Paid: $5–10k total, micro-influencers only** (10k–100k followers, $500–5k/post — [market analysis §6](../research/08-market-analysis.md)), niche-fit cozy accounts over big generalists, spent in two pulses: the Next Fest window and launch week. Vertical TikTok/Reels format targeting the female-majority cozy audience — the exact Hozy playbook (100k copies in 4 days).
- **What we hand creators structurally:** multi-day community projects give episodic arcs ("day 3 — the bridge is nearly done"); everyone's journal and sketches differ, so videos don't exhaust the game; 30–60 s self-contained bites are native to the design (one supper, one recipe, one zoom).
- **Licensor dependency (flagged again deliberately):** creator content rights and approval turnaround must be contractually confirmed before signing the licence. If the licensor requires per-asset approval slower than ~5 working days, the cadence and creator programme both break — negotiate a pre-approved asset-class list (in-engine footage, photo-mode exports, screenshots) instead of per-item approval.

## 10. Review-quality doctrine (≥90% positive)

Review score is the business multiplier, not a vanity metric: week-1 top sellers average **91% positive vs 67%** for underperformers, and the conversion spread it drives is 10–20× wide ([market analysis §3](../research/08-market-analysis.md)). Cozy audiences forgive scope and punish jank.

- **Target: ≥90% positive at day 7.** Every scope/date decision is checked against this number; ship smaller and flawless rather than bigger at 75%.
- **Mechanism, not hope:** the **3–6 month closed beta** (Q4 2027–Q2 2028, §5) runs a save-corruption and quest-blocker bounty focus, weekly full-playthrough tests, and min-spec/Steam Deck passes ([cozy pitfalls, risk 6](../research/09-cozy-pitfalls.md)). Steam Deck Verified and the 60 fps @ 1080p GTX-1060-class gate ([D2](../gdd/00-decisions.md)) are launch blockers.
- **"Every public milestone is a review event."** The demo, the fest build, and launch each get their own hardening pass; a buggy demo poisons the fest's 3×-better-converting wishlists.
- PC + Deck only at 1.0 (§12) exists partly *for* this doctrine: no console cert queue between us and a day-1 fix.
- No launch until the beta's last two weekly full playthroughs are blocker-free. This rule outranks the calendar; the wishlist slip rule (§5) already gives the schedule its escape valve.

## 11. Price & discount policy

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Base price | **$19.99** ([D11](../gdd/00-decisions.md)) | 25–35 h content clears the $4–5/hour complaint threshold; $15–20 maximises indie revenue ([market analysis §4](../research/08-market-analysis.md)) |
| Launch discount | **−10%** ($17.99), 1 week | Standard wishlist-conversion practice |
| Price ceiling | **$24.99 — never exceeded** | $25+ is publisher-scale content territory; never price above content ([synthesis §4, risk 5](../research/00-synthesis.md)) |
| Year-one discounts | **No deep discounts.** Max −20%, no more than two sale events (e.g. Wholesome sale, one seasonal Steam sale), none in the first 2 months | Protects early-buyer trust and the year-2 tail |
| Year two+ | −25 to −33% ceiling alongside the free seasonal update | Discount news rides content news |
| Regional pricing | Steam-recommended matrix, reviewed at launch | Especially zh/JA markets we localise for |
| Fallback price | $14.99 only if scope materially shrinks to the G4 fallback game | Price mirrors content honestly |
| Monetisation | **None beyond the base game — no FOMO, no MTX, ever** ([D11](../gdd/00-decisions.md)) | Genre-radioactive |

## 12. Platform sequencing

- **1.0: PC/Steam + Steam Deck Verified. Nothing else** ([D11](../gdd/00-decisions.md), [D5 Won't](../gdd/00-decisions.md)). Best discoverability, no cert, same-day patching while the review score is being won.
- **Switch 2 is the first console, +6–12 months after launch, and only post-recoup.** It is the natural cozy second home (A Little to the Left hit #2 on third-party eShop downloads during its surge). Indie ports run 1–7 months; outsource it or have a publisher fund it — a funded port is an explicitly acceptable publisher deal-sweetener *separate from* the revenue-share floor ([funding.md §6](./funding.md)).
- Day-and-date console launch happens only in the world where a publisher funds and project-manages it — we do not plan for it.
- Other PC storefronts: not before the Switch 2 decision; Steam's algorithm is the machine this whole plan feeds.

| Review % (post-launch) | Steam | ≥90% at day 7 |

## 14. The never-do list

No Early Access (closed playtest instead — community-progression pays off at 1.0, and every EA milestone is an extra review event to survive). No second Next Fest — there isn't one. No announce-to-launch window beyond 18 months. No canon names in public before signature. No launch under 25k wishlists. No launch into the November AAA wall. No deep discounts in year one. No paid mega-influencers. No trailer frame that reads as combat, timers, or fail states. No FOMO mechanics, ever.
