# Comps Research 06 — No-Combat Progression & Community/Emotional Quest Design

**Games analyzed:** Cozy Grove, Spiritfarer, Wylde Flowers, Eastshade, Garden Story, Ooblets.
**Question:** How do successful no-combat games structure progression, quests, multi-day pacing, and emotional payoff — and what should Brambly Hedge's community projects (mill restoration, bridge repair) copy or avoid?
**Researched:** July 2026.

## Key Takeaways

- **No-combat progression is always a braid of 3-4 gate types:** relationship gates (friendship unlocks), story gates (chapter beats), tool/ability gates (Spiritfarer's Icebreaker, Eastshade's zipline), and area gates (tolls, bridges, town level). Every comp uses at least three; none uses player levels as the primary axis.
- **The single most powerful trick in the genre is mechanical reciprocity:** in Spiritfarer, spirits you've made "Ecstatic" actively work for you (chop wood, fish, gift items, play mood-boosting music). Helping others must visibly help the player back — not just emit hearts.
- **Real-time gating (Cozy Grove) is the most polarizing design in the space.** It worked commercially and was praised by busy players, but Steam forums demanded store-page disclaimers and reviews said "come back tomorrow" robs autonomy. For a premium PC game, multi-day content should run on *in-game* days, never real days.
- **Spiritfarer's emotional power comes from agency + consequence:** the player chooses *when* to take a spirit to the Everdoor, and departed spirits leave Spirit Flowers that are required crafting materials for the boat upgrades that unlock the rest of the map — grief literally fuels progression.
- **Wylde Flowers proves full VA is a premium differentiator but a publisher-scale cost:** ~18 hours of dialogue, 350 cutscenes, 230 names in credits, ~3 years, a team that grew ~4x — enabled by Apple's money. Eastshade got 50 voice actors for ~$10k by keeping lines sparse and letting actors self-direct.
- **The most-reported failure mode is "busywork that pretends to be gameplay":** Ooblets' daily-capped wishies currency with huge sinks, Garden Story's tiny random request pool (same request 2-3x in a row), Spiritfarer's repeated crafting minigames in the back half. Repetition is tolerated only when it carries new narrative.
- **Visible world-state change is the reward that never fatigues:** Cozy Grove's island literally regains color as bears find peace; Eastshade opens bridges; Garden Story repairs roads. Reviewers criticize rebuilding that is "almost entirely aesthetic" — restored things must *function* (new NPC routines, new services, new recipes).
- **Story is the loot.** Cozy Grove explicitly splits quests into timed daily chores (no story) vs. untimed story quests (bear memories); players tolerate the chores because the story quests pay out narrative. Never attach zero narrative to a multi-step quest.
- **Let players trigger the big beats:** Wylde Flowers' seasons only advance when the player performs the coven ritual — a pacing valve praised as "reinforcing its cozy, relaxed pace." Same principle as choosing when to walk a spirit to the Everdoor.

## 1. Cozy Grove (Spry Fox, April 2021)

Context: Animal-Crossing-like about a ghost island of dead bear "Spirit Scouts." Spry Fox was acquired by Netflix in Oct 2022 ([Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/netflix-acquires-i-cozy-grove-i-developer-spry-fox)) and sold back to founders Daniel Cook and David Edery in Dec 2025 ([Shacknews](https://www.shacknews.com/article/147036/netflix-games-sells-spry-fox-to-founders)). A mobile sequel, Camp Spirit, shipped via Netflix in 2024.

### Progression without combat
- **Real-time clock, no time skip.** Each real day generates ~20-30 minutes of content: new bear quests, forageables, fish ([Switchblade guide](https://www.switchbladegaming.com/cozy-games/cozy-grove-guide/)).
- **Two quest classes** ([Cozy Grove wiki](https://cozygrove.fandom.com/wiki/Quest_(Cozy_Grove))): *timed daily quests* (expire, no story content) and *untimed story quests* (advance a bear's memories/arc). This split is the game's core taxonomy.
- **Island-as-progress-bar:** feeding spirit logs to Flamey (campfire) physically expands the island; helping bears returns color to grayscale zones — progression is rendered as world-state, not numbers ([Vice](https://www.vice.com/en/article/cozy-grove-is-ok-telling-players-to-log-off-its-delightful/), [Switchblade](https://www.switchbladegaming.com/cozy-games/cozy-grove-guide/)).
- Full story arc: roughly **80-100 real days** of daily play, ~35-50 hours total ([Switchblade](https://www.switchbladegaming.com/cozy-games/cozy-grove-guide/)).

### Quest taxonomy (concrete)
Forage-and-deliver (branches, shells), craft-and-deliver, cook-and-deliver, catch-specific-fish, find-buried-and-place (spirit remembers where something was hidden). Bears only offer a new quest if the player has none active from them ([Switchblade](https://www.switchbladegaming.com/cozy-games/cozy-grove-guide/), [Cozy Grove wiki](https://cozygrove.fandom.com/wiki/Quest_(Cozy_Grove))).

### The real-time drip — did players like it?
- **Designer rationale** (studio head David Edery): it's "less about placing limits on the player so much as pacing the narrative content so that we deliver an enjoyable episodic experience over months of time"; he compared it to Disney+ weekly episodes vs. Netflix binging, and wants players to feel "you've accomplished something today and have something to look forward to tomorrow" ([Vice](https://www.vice.com/en/article/cozy-grove-is-ok-telling-players-to-log-off-its-delightful/), [Siliconera interview](https://www.siliconera.com/interview-spry-fox-explains-how-animal-crossing-shaped-cozy-grove/)).
- **Positive reception:** praised as a game that "gives a damn about players" and respects time ([TNW](https://thenextweb.com/news/review-cozy-grove-is-what-happens-when-game-developers-give-a-damn-about-players)); many players loved the 20-30 min ritual with zero FOMO pressure.
- **Negative reception:** a top Steam thread demands Spry Fox "put a disclaimer on all game descriptions" about real-time gating ([Steam](https://steamcommunity.com/app/1458100/discussions/0/3172198151258336209/)); reviews complained the game "tells players to come back tomorrow," robbing autonomy, and that after ~30 minutes there is nothing satisfying left ([Destructoid](https://www.destructoid.com/reviews/review-cozy-grove/), [GameGrin](https://www.gamegrin.com/reviews/cozy-grove-review/)). Guides exist specifically for "what to do after your daily quests" — evidence of the content wall ([TheGamer](https://www.thegamer.com/cozy-grove-things-to-do-after-daily-quests/)).
- **Verdict:** the drip works when it matches marketing and audience expectation ("a game you sip"), but it hard-caps engaged players, and the studio itself moved to a more conventional energy model in the mobile sequel. It is a positioning decision, not a free win.

## 2. Spiritfarer (Thunder Lotus, Aug 2020)

Context: "cozy management game about dying." 500k copies in first ~5 weeks ([Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/thunder-lotus-cozy-management-sim-i-spiritfarer-i-has-topped-500-000-sales)), 1M+ by the free Farewell Edition (2021) ([Thunder Lotus press release](https://thunderlotusgames.com/press-releases/spiritfarer-farewell-edition-press/)). GDC 2021 talk: ["Spiritfarer's Journey: Crafting Tenderness"](https://gdcvault.com/play/1027409/-Spiritfarer-s-Journey-Crafting).

### Progression without combat
- **Area gates on the sea map:** ice, rock, and mist walls require three boat upgrades — Icebreaker, Rock Destroyer, Mist Cleaner 1000 — all mandatory ([TheGamer ship upgrades](https://www.thegamer.com/spiritfarer-ship-upgrade-guide/)).
- **Ability gates:** traversal moves (double jump, zipline, bounce, dash) bought at shrines for 2 Obols each; Obols come from the spirits themselves. Ability shrines are themselves behind boat gates (zipline at Nordweiler needs Icebreaker, dash at Greymist Peaks needs Mist Cleaner) — a braided dependency chain ([TheGamer shrines](https://www.thegamer.com/spiritfarer-every-shrine-upgrade-in-the-game-and-where-to-find-it/)).
- **Emotionally-charged materials:** boat upgrades require Spirit Flowers, which are given only when a spirit departs at the Everdoor (e.g., Rock Destroyer needs 2 Spirit Flowers; Mist Cleaner needs 3) ([Guide Strats](https://guidestrats.com/spiritfarer-boat-improvements/)). The player must let friends go to progress — mechanics and theme are the same object.
- **Building progression:** each recruited spirit demands their own cabin + facilities (garden, kitchen, loom, foundry), so the boat physically grows with the community.

### Quest structure per spirit (the "Arc" pattern)
Recruit on a specific island (often behind a gate) → build their house → learn food likes/dislikes, fulfill personal requests → escort through bespoke events (multi-stage set pieces) → the spirit asks to go to the Everdoor → **player chooses when** → personalized farewell cutscene. Academic analysis highlights that the game never forces the goodbye, mirroring "unfinished business" psychology ([Springer analysis](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11423-024-10357-x), [Fandom essay](https://www.fandom.com/articles/spiritfarer-and-the-gamification-of-grief)).

### How helping is made mechanically rewarding (the standout system)
Mood system: food they love, hugs, conversation, and better housing raise spirits toward Ecstatic. Happy/Ecstatic spirits *actively produce*: Atul plays music (+3 mood aura to everyone) and chops wood/mans the sawmill; Astrid fishes for you; Gwen harvests fibers; Gustav makes sushi; Buck gifts snacks and XP potions ([TheGamer perks list](https://www.thegamer.com/spiritfarer-spirit-location-perks-likes-dislikes/), [Spiritfarer wiki: Mood](https://spiritfarer.fandom.com/wiki/Mood)). Care in → labor and gifts out. This closes the loop that most cozy games leave open.

### Emotional storytelling techniques
- The Everdoor: a half-circle bridge reflected in water, deliberately "a symbolic place of respite and grandeur, neither fear-inducing nor too whimsical" ([Game Developer interview](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/inside-the-thoughtful-design-of-thunder-lotus-i-spiritfarer-i-)).
- The hug was treated as "a core part of the game's heart," with dedicated animation direction — a mechanical verb for tenderness (same source).
- Each spirit pairs an animal form with a flower symbol; characters were written from multi-page bios so the late-arc dialogue lands as a coherent life story.
- Music shifts from ambient support to "sweeping and epic" at departures — the score marks emotional structure.

### Criticisms
"Too mired in frustrating busywork" — repeated micro-minigames (bellows, crushing seeds, hammering glass) scale up with recipe complexity; the second half drags; progression sometimes hangs on vague triggers; the sea dangles visibly-locked objectives long before they're achievable ([NME review](https://www.nme.com/reviews/game-reviews/spiritfarer-review-a-beautiful-management-game-about-dying-too-mired-in-frustrating-busywork-2732038), [Metacritic user reviews](https://www.metacritic.com/game/spiritfarer/user-reviews/)).

## 3. Wylde Flowers (Studio Drydock, Apple Arcade Feb 2022; Switch/Steam Sept 20, 2022)

Context: farming/witchcraft life sim; Apple Design Award for Inclusivity 2022 ([GamesHub](https://www.gameshub.com/news/news/wylde-flowers-inclusivity-apple-design-awards-20772/)) and Apple Arcade Game of the Year 2022 ([Press-Start](https://press-start.com.au/news/nintendo/2022/11/30/wylde-flowers-has-won-the-app-stores-apple-arcade-game-of-the-year/)).

### Full voice acting — cost/benefit (primary source: [Apple "Behind the Design"](https://developer.apple.com/news/?id=din5adp5))
- **Scale:** ~18 hours of dialogue, 350 cutscenes, ~30 voiced characters, 230 names in the credits, ~3 years of development; script "a little longer than War and Peace." Initial team ~12, grew roughly fourfold.
- **Funding reality:** VO and cinematics were *not* in the original plan — they became possible only when Apple became publisher. This is the honest budget lesson for a 1-3 person team.
- **Craft details:** named actors (Valerie Rose Lohman, Erika Ishii, BAFTA-winner Cissy Jones); actors improvised and requested rewrites over Slack; 7 differently-acted proposal scenes for different suitors.
- **Knock-on design effect:** early top-down camera "felt too disconnected from the characters, especially in a game with so much acting," so they moved to an angled, closer camera — directly relevant precedent for our Storybook Isometric Camera with cinematic framing at key moments.
- **Payoff:** VA is the game's headline differentiator in nearly every review and drove awards ([GamesRadar interview](https://www.gamesradar.com/how-studio-drydock-created-one-of-the-most-memorable-and-welcoming-farming-sims-ive-ever-played-good-representation-was-integral/)).

### Progression without combat
- **Story-gated year:** main story spans one in-game year, ~30 hours; each season turn is a *story beat* triggered by the player performing a coven ritual — seasons last as long as the player wants ([Screen Rant](https://screenrant.com/wylde-flowers-game-review/), [Cozy Game Reviews](https://cozygamereviews.com/wylde-flowers-review/)). This is the genre's best pacing valve: calendar pressure removed, narrative structure kept.
- **Dual quest tracks:** town storyline (prejudice against the coven, a mystery to unravel) + per-character relationship arcs, all fully voiced; farming/crafting exists mainly to feed the story economy.
- **Criticisms:** farming too shallow for sim veterans; late-game daily tasks repetitive; magic progression occasionally throttled by resource bottlenecks ([Cozy Game Reviews](https://cozygamereviews.com/wylde-flowers-review/)).

## 4. Eastshade (Eastshade Studios, Feb 2019)

Context: first-person open world where you are a *painter*, zero combat. Two core devs (Danny Weinbaum + Jaclyn), <$100k in contractors, ~$200k budget over ~5-6 years; ~$2M gross / ~$1.1M net within ~1.5 years ([official postmortem, Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/postmortem-eastshade)).

### Progression without combat
- **Inspiration economy:** each painting costs Inspiration; it replenishes only by discovering new locations, reading books, hearing stories, completing quests — curiosity is the XP ([PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/eastshade-review/), [Heterogenous Tasks essay](https://heterogenoustasks.wordpress.com/2019/05/15/eastshade-fantasy-without-crisis/)).
- **Layered civic gates:** bridge tolls (currency), letters of recommendation (requires befriending specific NPCs), sequential quest chains (get sealant → learn password → find amulet → unlock zipline). Social standing functions as the key ring ([Heterogenous Tasks](https://heterogenoustasks.wordpress.com/2019/05/15/eastshade-fantasy-without-crisis/)).
- **Quest types:** painting commissions with compositional constraints ("paint X location with Y in frame"), deliveries, item finding, small mysteries — "simple, familiar forms of NPC quest" made fresh because the reward is access and knowledge, not power ([PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/eastshade-review/)).

### Lessons from the postmortem ([Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/postmortem-eastshade))
- They cut survival meters early: "constantly depleting bars were entirely at odds with how we wanted the game to feel." (Directly applicable: resist stamina/hunger bars in Brambly Hedge.)
- **VA on a shoestring:** ~50 voice actors for ~$10k, self-directed remote recording — partial VA is absolutely reachable for a tiny team.
- Kept the painting *mechanic* simple deliberately, spending effort on "interesting quests with unique characters" instead.
- Built the opening region (Lyndow) as vertical slice/tutorial first.

## 5. Garden Story (Picogram / Rose City Games, Aug 2021)

Context: village-restoration action-RPG (note: has light combat — included here for its town-progression systems).

### Town/community progression
- Requests come from village elders, **daily bulletin boards**, and villager favors, in three categories: conflict, maintenance, forage. Completing them earns **town XP by category**; category levels upgrade shop inventories, and **main story is gated behind town level** ([RPGFan](https://www.rpgfan.com/review/garden-story/)).
- Player growth is via found stat gems and equippable "memories" (perk system themed as remembering experiences) — flavor-aligned with community/nostalgia rather than levels ([RPGFan](https://www.rpgfan.com/review/garden-story/)).

### Criticisms — a compact anti-pattern list
- Requests draw from **a small random pool, so players repeat the identical task 2-3 times in short succession** — the single most cited flaw ([RPGFan](https://www.rpgfan.com/review/garden-story/), [Nintendo Life](https://www.nintendolife.com/reviews/switch-eshop/garden_story)).
- Gardening and building — the fantasy on the box — were "more of an afterthought," "almost entirely aesthetic" ([RPGFan](https://www.rpgfan.com/review/garden-story/)). Restoration that doesn't change function reads as hollow.

## 6. Ooblets (Glumberland, EA July 2020, 1.0 Sept 1, 2022)

Context: farming + creature-collection with dance battles instead of combat; 2-person core team funded by an Epic exclusivity deal ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ooblets), [Kotaku](https://kotaku.com/ooblets-early-access-switch-september-release-date-1849431109)).

### Progression without combat
- **Region unlocks via the Mayor's checklist:** Mayor Tinstle's tasks (win a dance battle / collect resources) open new districts ([Ooblets wiki](https://ooblets.fandom.com/wiki/How_To_Play_Guide_For_Ooblets)).
- **Wishies economy:** daily tasks grant up to ~35 wishies/day, spent at the Wishy Well on shop expansions, new ooblet slots, and upgrades; badges (achievements) also award wishies; a soft character level unlocks more tasks and Well entries ([TheGamer wishies guide](https://www.thegamer.com/ooblets-obtain-spend-wishie-guide/)).

### Criticisms — the cautionary tale of the six
- Game Informer titled its review "A Cheerful Grind": "tedious, grind-heavy progression that tests your patience" ([Game Informer](https://gameinformer.com/review/ooblets/a-cheerful-grind)).
- Reviewers "spent and spent and spent wishies on necessary perks... and by the time I was through with the grinding I didn't care anymore" — daily-capped currency + mandatory big sinks = forced multi-day stretching that players experience as padding ([Hey Poor Player](https://www.heypoorplayer.com/2022/09/01/ooblets-review-switch/)).
- "Commits the cardinal sin of cosy sims: busywork that pretends to be gameplay... nothing meaningful to do except wait for crops to grow, resources to respawn, or NPCs to stop gatekeeping the next quest" ([NAG](https://www.nag.co.za/2025/05/15/ooblets-review-warm-hugs-and-dance-battles/)). Post-launch patches added more wishy sources to soften this.

## 7. Cross-game synthesis: making "help others" mechanically rewarding

1. **Reciprocity loop (best-in-class: Spiritfarer):** helped NPCs produce labor, gifts, buffs, and ambient joy (music auras). The player's kindness compounds into economy.
2. **World-state transformation (Cozy Grove, Eastshade, Garden Story):** color returns, bridges open, shops stock more. Must be *functional*, not just cosmetic (Garden Story's failure).
3. **Narrative disclosure as loot (Cozy Grove, Spiritfarer, Wylde Flowers):** each quest stage pays out a memory, a secret, a voiced scene. Cozy Grove proves players will do chores if story quests are clearly the "real" reward track.
4. **Social standing as key ring (Eastshade):** friendship literally unlocks doors (letters of recommendation). Elegant no-combat gating.
5. **Emotionally-charged resources (Spiritfarer):** Spirit Flowers make the theme load-bearing in the economy. Rare, powerful trick.
6. **Agency over the emotional beat (Spiritfarer's Everdoor, Wylde Flowers' season rituals):** the player pulls the trigger on climaxes; the game never yanks them forward.

## 8. Pattern library for community-driven quests

### Structures that work
- **The Arc** (Spiritfarer): meet → house/settle → learn preferences → personal requests → bespoke event(s) → player-triggered resolution → permanent trace left in world. Best for character-centric chains.
- **The Chapter Chain** (Cozy Grove): one NPC, many chapters, each gated by friendship + a small task; untimed; interleaved with optional dailies. Best for long-haul relationship content.
- **The Access Chain** (Eastshade): physical gate → the key is social (befriend, help, learn) → new region → new stories. Best for map pacing without combat.
- **The Season Beat** (Wylde Flowers): season/story advances only when the player performs a ritual/festival; content within a season is untimed. Best top-level pacing spine.
- **The Town Ledger** (Garden Story): categorized contribution points level the town and upgrade services — workable as a *supporting* visualization, dangerous as the main loop (feels like a spreadsheet if tasks repeat).
- **The Mayor's List** (Ooblets): explicit checklist gating regions — functional but transactional; use for onboarding at most.

### Pacing rules extracted
1. Multi-day waits must be **diegetic and visible** (scaffolding rises, thatch appears, NPCs seen working on-site), never an opaque countdown.
2. After the day's structured content, always leave **voluntary open-ended verbs** (fishing, photography, decorating, wandering) — Cozy Grove's "soft wall" complaints came from having nothing self-directed to do.
3. **In-game days, not real days**, for a premium PC title; if any real-time element survives, disclose it on the store page (Cozy Grove's forum revolt).
4. A helped NPC should **give something back within one in-game day** (gift on doorstep, help gathering, a discount, a new dish) — instant reciprocity, delayed transformation.
5. Emotional climaxes are **player-triggered** with generous "not yet" affordance.
6. Big projects end in **celebration punctuation** (festival/feast), then a **function change** the next morning.

### Anti-patterns (each observed in reviews above)
- Daily-capped currency feeding large mandatory sinks (Ooblets wishies).
- Small random request pools → visible repetition (Garden Story).
- Micro-minigames repeated dozens of times as crafting "texture" (Spiritfarer bellows/seed-crushing).
- Vague progression triggers late-game; players don't know what unlocks the next beat (Spiritfarer second half).
- Cosmetic-only restoration; rebuilt structures that do nothing (Garden Story).
- Hard daily walls with "come back tomorrow" messaging (Cozy Grove negative reviews).
- Fetch chains with zero narrative payload attached (universal complaint; Cozy Grove dailies tolerated only because the story track exists beside them).
- Dangling visibly-locked objectives many hours before they're actionable (Spiritfarer's gated seas).

## Implications for Brambly Hedge: The Game

**1. Structure community projects as Spiritfarer-style Arcs run on in-game time.** Recommended template for the mill restoration: **Proposal** (community meeting scene, journal page opens) → **Contribution** (2-4 in-game days: gather/craft/repair subtasks; each participating NPC visibly contributes per their schedule) → **Construction** (visible staged build over 2-3 in-game days; player can lend a hand daily for bonus scenes) → **Celebration** (festival/feast, book-style set-piece framed by the storybook camera) → **Function** (mill grinds flour from day one after: new recipes, a miller's new routine, carts on the path). Total: roughly 5-8 in-game days per major project, 3-5 phases. Never gate a phase on real-world time.

**2. Copy Spiritfarer's reciprocity, reskinned gently.** Helped mice should: leave baked goods at your door, hum/whistle near you (ambient mood), gather alongside you, teach recipes, open shortcuts. Budget rule: every completed favor produces at least one tangible give-back within one in-game day.

**3. Make friendship the key ring (Eastshade).** Use "letters of introduction," invitations, and being vouched-for as area/content gates (the Old Oak Palace cellars open because Mrs. Apple trusts you) instead of tool-stat checks alone. Combine with 3-5 tool gates (boat, ladder, lantern, winter clothing) for map pacing.

**4. Adopt Wylde Flowers' player-triggered season beat, hybridized.** Seasons advance only after the seasonal festival is held, and the festival becomes available once its community project + a light story beat are done. This gives us Wylde Flowers' pressure-free pacing while keeping our four-festivals spine. Target one season ≈ 6-10 hours of unhurried play.

**5. Split quests Cozy-Grove-style, but soften the timer.** Two tracks: *story/community quests* (untimed, always narrative payload) and *small favors* (light, optional, refresh daily from a **hand-authored pool of 60+ variants minimum** — Garden Story shows small random pools get caught immediately). Avoid expiry except for gentle seasonal availability ("blackberries only in autumn").

**6. Voice acting: take the Eastshade route now, keep the Wylde Flowers route as a publisher-funded stretch.** Plan for partial VA: ~10-15k budget for 30-50 self-directed remote actors covering key story scenes and festival moments, plus expressive vocalizations elsewhere. Full VA at Wylde scale (18h dialogue, 350 cutscenes) required a platform publisher and ~4x team growth — out of reach for 1-3 people. Note Wylde Flowers' camera lesson supports our design: acted scenes demand the camera to move close, which is exactly the cinematic-framing feature of the Storybook Camera.

**7. No depleting meters.** Eastshade cut survival bars because they fought the feel; Ooblets' waiting-on-meters was its worst-reviewed trait. No hunger/stamina; use daylight and season as the only soft clocks.

**8. Render progression as world detail, not numbers.** Our equivalent of Cozy Grove's returning color: repaired thatch, relit windows, busier paths, more birdsong, NPC furniture upgrades visible through dollhouse interiors. Every community project must change at least: one NPC schedule, one service/economy node, and one ambient/visual layer.

**9. Emotional beats: use Spiritfarer's techniques at Brambly Hedge intensity.** We don't need death; the books' peaks are weddings, births, rescues, and elders' stories (mirror *Poppy's Babies*, *The Secret Staircase*, *Winter Story*). Borrow: a dedicated tenderness verb (a hug/paw-clasp equivalent), leitmotif music that swells only at ceremonies, one symbolic location for milestone scenes (our Everdoor-equivalent could be the hornbeam tree or the bridge), and player-chosen timing for ceremony scenes.

**10. Session shape target:** one in-game day ≈ one comfortable 20-40 minute session containing: 1 story/community beat, 2-3 small favors, 1 project contribution, plus unlimited freeform activity — Cozy Grove's "accomplished something today, something to look forward to tomorrow" without its hard wall.
