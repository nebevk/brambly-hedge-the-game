# Technical Research: The Storybook Isometric Camera in Unity

Research date: 2026-07-04. Scope: Cinemachine 2.x vs 3.x, camera architecture patterns, roof/wall fade and occlusion techniques, projection choice, rotation/input UX, pitfalls, and a concrete recommended architecture + MVP test plan for Brambly Hedge: The Game (Unity 2022.3.50f1, built-in RP today, tiny team).

## Key Takeaways

- **Use Cinemachine 3.1.x, not 2.x.** CM3's minimum Unity version is exactly our 2022.3 LTS, and since we have no legacy CM2 content there is zero upgrade cost — while starting on 2.x would guarantee a painful migration later (breaking API: new `Unity.Cinemachine` namespace, `CinemachineCamera` replaces `CinemachineVirtualCamera`/`FreeLook`, no more `m_` fields, native Splines replace CM paths) ([install/compat](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.cinemachine@3.1/manual/InstallationAndUpgrade.html), [upgrade guide](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.cinemachine@3.1/manual/CinemachineUpgradeFrom2.html)).
- **Perspective projection with a narrow FOV (~25–35°), not orthographic.** Tiny Glade is fully perspective (adjustable FOV, fish-eye option, even a first-person mode) and gets its diorama feel from depth of field + lighting, not from orthographic flatness ([GameRant camera guide](https://gamerant.com/how-to-control-the-camera-in-tiny-glade/), [80.lv first-person](https://80.lv/articles/cozy-castle-builder-tiny-glade-now-has-a-first-person-view-mode)). Orthographic buys nothing in a full-3D Unity scene and creates pixel-jitter problems under rotation ([Unity discussion](https://discussions.unity.com/t/pixel-perfect-camera-jitter-with-isometric-camera-rotation-solutions/910674), [ortho tips](https://thinkinginsideadifferentbox.wordpress.com/2020/09/27/orthographic_camera_tips_for_unity3d/)).
- **The core architecture pattern is "one Brain, many CinemachineCameras, priority-driven by trigger volumes."** The Brain always blends to the most recently activated camera with equal/higher priority; zone triggers just bump priority or enable a composed vcam ([NotSlot control-zones tutorial](https://notslot.com/tutorials/2020/03/25d-game-in-unity-part-5), [Medium trigger tutorial](https://medium.com/@austinjy13/using-triggers-to-change-cameras-unity-cinemachine-fb4825fa1885)). ClearShot and State-Driven Camera are available but are overkill for our MVP.
- **Do NOT use Cinemachine's Deoccluder/Collider for occlusion** — its only strategy is moving the camera to preserve line of sight, which destroys a fixed isometric composition ([Deoccluder docs](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.cinemachine@3.1/manual/CinemachineDeoccluder.html)). Fade the occluders instead.
- **Dithered (screen-door) transparency is the industry-standard fade for roofs and occluding plants**: objects stay in the opaque queue, write depth, cast shadows, and avoid all transparency sorting problems; it's the same mechanism as Unity's LOD crossfade. An 8×8 Bayer matrix reduces banding ([Daniel Ilett URP dither](https://danielilett.com/2020-04-19-tut5-5-urp-dither-transparency/), [gkjohnson built-in RP shader, MIT](https://github.com/gkjohnson/unity-dithered-transparency-shader)).
- **For the player disappearing behind hedges/plants: screen-space cutout circle + dither**, driven by a C# raycast from camera to player that sets shader properties — a solved, documented pattern ([Daniel Ilett wall cutout](https://danielilett.com/2021-03-19-tut5-15-wall-cutout/)). Sims 4's "Cutaway" mode (hide all walls between the current view and the back wall, plus cut to the active Sim) is the behavioral reference for interiors ([Carl's Sims 4 guide](https://www.carls-sims-4-guide.com/forum/index.php?topic=21364.0)).
- **Rotation UX: snap in 45° steps with a fast eased tween; only allow "decisions" at snap positions.** Monument Valley's team only created interaction decision points when geometry was at a snap position ([Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/making-the-impossible-possible-in-i-monument-valley-i-)); Tunic ships an entirely fixed camera and still works ([Worthplaying review](https://worthplaying.com/article/2022/3/16/reviews/131106-pc-review-tunic/)); Tiny Glade patch 1.11.2 had to add "rotate/zoom around screen center" as an alternative to cursor-pivot because players found cursor-pivot disorienting ([patch notes](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/2198150/view/543346166601876515)).
- **Motion-comfort rule: translate freely, rotate carefully.** Animated camera *rotation* is the main motion-sickness trigger; translation and smoothed, damped moves are fine. Keep arrival "cinematic reframes" mostly translational, slow, and interruptible ([Busseneau on motion sickness](https://nicolas.busseneau.fr/en/blog/2020/09/alleviating-motion-sickness-in-first-person-video-games), [CGCookie game camera](https://cgcookie.com/posts/creating-a-great-game-camera)).
- **Move to URP now, during pre-production.** Shader Graph on built-in RP is "compatibility only" and receives no feature updates; every fade/cutout/tilt-shift shader we need is URP-first in tutorials and tooling ([Unity discussion + docs](https://discussions.unity.com/t/does-shader-graph-support-built-in-rendering/700421), [Shader Graph docs](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.shadergraph@14.0/manual/Getting-Started.html)).

## 1. Cinemachine 2.x vs 3.x — which one, and what changed

**Compatibility.** Cinemachine 3.x requires **Unity 2022.3 LTS or later** ([installation docs](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.cinemachine@3.1/manual/InstallationAndUpgrade.html)). Cinemachine 2.10.x is the last 2.x line and is the "verified" package Unity 2022.3's Package Manager offers by default (2.10.1+ verified for 2022.3 — [2.10 changelog](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.cinemachine@2.10/changelog/CHANGELOG.html)). Unity 6 ships with CM 3.1.x as the default. So on 2022.3 we genuinely have the choice; on Unity 6 the choice is made for us.

**CM3 breaking changes vs 2.x** ([upgrade guide](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.cinemachine@3.1/manual/CinemachineUpgradeFrom2.html), [Unity blog: What's new in CM3](https://unity.com/blog/engine-platform/see-whats-new-with-cinemachine-3)):

| Cinemachine 2.x | Cinemachine 3.x |
|---|---|
| namespace `Cinemachine` | `Unity.Cinemachine` (Utility folded in) |
| `CinemachineVirtualCamera`, `CinemachineFreeLook` | `CinemachineCamera` (one class for both) |
| `m_FieldName` public fields | `FieldName` (all `m_` prefixes removed) |
| pipeline on hidden child GO "cm" | pipeline components directly on the `CinemachineCamera` GameObject, accessed via plain `GetComponent()` |
| `CinemachineFramingTransposer` | `CinemachinePositionComposer` |
| `CinemachineOrbitalTransposer` | `CinemachineOrbitalFollow` |
| `CinemachineTransposer` | `CinemachineFollow` |
| `CinemachineComposer` | `CinemachineRotationComposer` |
| `CinemachinePath`/`SmoothPath` + `TrackedDolly` | Unity native **SplineContainer** + `CinemachineSplineDolly` / `CinemachineSplineCart` |
| `CinemachineCollider` | `CinemachineDeoccluder`; new `CinemachineDecollider`; `Confiner` split into 2D/3D |
| vcam filtering via Unity layers/culling mask | dedicated **Cinemachine Channels** (stops wasting Unity layers) |
| per-vcam/Brain events | **global events** on `CinemachineCore` (+ listener behaviors) |

CM3 also decoupled user input from camera components (input goes through an external axis-controller layer, plays nicely with the new Input System) and lets ClearShot use custom shot-quality evaluators ([what's new](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.cinemachine@3.1/manual/whats-new.html)).

**Verdict for us:** new project, no CM2 assets to port → **CM 3.1.x from day one**. All new learning material, samples, and fixes target 3.x; the 2→3 migration is described by Unity itself as "will likely require some effort" ([needle-mirror README](https://github.com/needle-mirror/com.unity.cinemachine)) — effort we can simply never pay.

## 2. Architecture patterns for zone-based storybook framing

**Pattern A — vcam-per-zone + trigger volumes (recommended core).** All cameras share a default priority; a trigger collider around a key location activates/raises the priority of a hand-composed `CinemachineCamera`, and the Brain blends to it. "Cinemachine Brain chooses the most recently activated Virtual Camera with the same or higher priority as the live Virtual Camera" — the whole system is just priorities + enablement ([NotSlot 2.5D control zones](https://notslot.com/tutorials/2020/03/25d-game-in-unity-part-5), [Medium: Using Triggers to Change Cameras](https://medium.com/@austinjy13/using-triggers-to-change-cameras-unity-cinemachine-fb4825fa1885)). Blend timing/easing is defined per camera-pair in a **Custom Blends asset** on the Brain, so "arrival at the mill" can get its own 2-second EaseInOut while everything else uses a 0.5s default.

**Pattern B — ClearShot for tricky spots.** `CinemachineClearShot` is a manager camera that owns several child vcams and automatically makes live whichever child reports the best shot quality (children evaluate obstruction/framing via their collider/deoccluder extensions); custom blends between children are supported, and CM3 allows custom shot-quality evaluators ([ClearShot docs](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.cinemachine@2.3/manual/CinemachineClearShot.html), [what's new in CM3](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.cinemachine@3.1/manual/whats-new.html)). Useful later for interiors with two valid compositions (e.g., kitchen vs pantry side of Crabapple Cottage); skip for MVP.

**Pattern C — State-Driven Camera.** Activates child vcams from Animator states, with per-state wait/hold times and custom blends ([docs](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.cinemachine@2.2/manual/CinemachineStateDrivenCamera.html)). Designed around animation states, not world zones — we'd be fighting it; our "states" (exterior/interior/arrival) are better modeled as a tiny explicit C# state machine choosing which vcam set is enabled.

**Pattern D — Spline dolly for arrival shots.** In CM3, dolly moves use Unity's native splines: author a short `SplineContainer` path (e.g., a gentle 2–3 m descent curving toward the mill façade), put `CinemachineSplineDolly` on the arrival vcam, and animate the position along the spline (or use damped auto-dolly) while a `CinemachineRotationComposer` keeps the landmark framed. Because it's still a vcam, the Brain blend in/out is free, and gameplay never has to pause — this is exactly the "gentle dolly to a composed shot" in our spec.

**Confinement:** `CinemachineConfiner3D` (CM3) keeps blends and player-follow inside a volume so compositions can't drift off the handcrafted set — Tiny Glade similarly restricts its camera "by the limits of the glade" ([GameRant](https://gamerant.com/how-to-control-the-camera-in-tiny-glade/)).

## 3. Projection: perspective vs orthographic, and the diorama feel

- **Tiny Glade is perspective**, verified: its photo mode exposes an adjustable field of view with a "Fish Eye" option, DoF focus picking, and the 2025 update added a full first-person walking mode — none of which exist in an orthographic renderer ([GameRant camera guide](https://gamerant.com/how-to-control-the-camera-in-tiny-glade/), [80.lv](https://80.lv/articles/cozy-castle-builder-tiny-glade-now-has-a-first-person-view-mode)). Its "miniature" charm comes from soft custom global illumination, DoF, and scale cues, not projection ([80.lv interview with Pounce Light](https://80.lv/articles/exclusive-tiny-glade-developers-discuss-bevy-proceduralism-publishers-cozy-games)).
- **Orthographic downsides in full 3D:** the scene looks flat; you "rig everything up as though it's full 3D perspective and then filter it through an unorthodox reproduction" ([orthographic camera tips](https://thinkinginsideadifferentbox.wordpress.com/2020/09/27/orthographic_camera_tips_for_unity3d/)); rotating an ortho isometric camera produces visible vertex/pixel jitter that developers struggle to fix ([Unity discussion](https://discussions.unity.com/t/pixel-perfect-camera-jitter-with-isometric-camera-rotation-solutions/910674)). Ortho's genuine benefits (readability of many units at once) serve RTS/tactics games, not us ([O'Reilly, Learning 2D Game Development](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/learning-2d-game/9780133523416/ch04lev2sec05.html)).
- **A narrow-FOV perspective camera (~20–35°) placed far away approximates the isometric look** while keeping parallax, DoF, and natural roof-fade raycasts — the standard compromise in modern "iso-style" 3D games (Fabledom, BG3 both do this).
- **Tilt-shift/diorama DoF:** tilt-shift is a specialized depth of field that defocuses a screen band (vertical or radial) rather than using a true focus plane; blurring top and bottom of frame is what sells the miniature illusion ([Unity legacy Tilt Shift docs](https://docs.unity3d.com/540/Documentation/Manual/script-TiltShiftHdr.html), [SC Post Effects docs](https://staggart.xyz/unity/sc-post-effects/scpe-docs/?section=tilt-shift)). In URP it's a small custom Renderer Feature (screen-position-based blur; depth-aware variants are higher quality) ([Noveltech URP tilt-shift](https://www.noveltech.dev/tilt-shift-unity)). Caution from photography practice: strong tilt-shift makes things read as *toys*; Brambly Hedge should whisper it (mild blur, only at closer zoom bands) so mice stay characters, not miniatures ([tilt-shift primer](https://blog.pond5.com/20031-making-a-tilt-shift-miniature-effect-video-practically-and-in-post/)).
- **Reference check — Snufkin: Melody of Moominvalley** achieves "storybook come to life" with a mostly bird's-eye/top-down camera plus a handful of layered vista cutscenes, in rich watercolor ([Adventure Game Hotspot review](https://adventuregamehotspot.com/review/1472/snufkin-melody-of-moominvalley), [GamingTrend](https://gamingtrend.com/reviews/snufkin-melody-of-moominvalley-review-embark-on-a-whimsical-adventure-of-melodies-and-moomins/)). Lesson: the storybook feel is carried by art direction and *composed moments* — reviewers explicitly wished the game left its top-down view more often. Our arrival-shot system directly answers that wish.

## 4. Roof/wall fade and the exterior→interior transition

**Behavioral reference — The Sims 4** has three wall modes: Walls Up, Walls Down, and **Cutaway** — "hide all walls between your current view and the back wall of your home, as well as cutaway to the active Sim if they happen to be behind the back wall"; floors above the current view level are hidden entirely ([Carl's Sims 4 guide](https://www.carls-sims-4-guide.com/forum/index.php?topic=21364.0), [SimsVIP build mode](https://simsvip.com/2014/08/06/the-sims-4-build-mode-lessons/)). Divinity: Original Sin 2 is widely cited as the reference for automatic roof/wall fade in isometric RPGs, though Larian never published an implementation writeup; player observation notes it fades the *first* occluding layer ([Larian forums](https://forums.larian.com/ubbthreads.php?ubb=showflat&Number=489086), [Steam discussion](https://steamcommunity.com/app/435150/discussions/0/343787920120053025/)).

**Implementation options, ranked for us:**

1. **Renderer-group fade with dithered transparency (recommended).** Author each building as separate mesh groups: `RoofShell`, `UpperWalls`, `Interior`. On door-trigger enter, tween a per-group `_FadeAlpha` (via `MaterialPropertyBlock`) on a dither-fade shader. Dither/screen-door transparency clips pixels in the opaque pass, so faded roofs still write depth, still cast shadows, never Z-sort against foliage, and cost less than alpha blending; it's the same technique as Unity's LOD crossfade, and an 8×8 dither matrix instead of 4×4 (16→64 levels) removes visible banding ([Daniel Ilett URP dither tutorial](https://danielilett.com/2020-04-19-tut5-5-urp-dither-transparency/), [Shader Graph Dither node](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.shadergraph@6.9/manual/Dither-Node.html), [Unity discussion on dither crossfade](https://discussions.unity.com/t/make-every-shader-have-a-dither-crossfade-effect-hdrp/898140)). A ready MIT reference for built-in RP exists ([gkjohnson/unity-dithered-transparency-shader](https://github.com/gkjohnson/unity-dithered-transparency-shader)); for URP it's ~6 Shader Graph nodes.
2. **Hard renderer swap / layer culling** (roof `SetActive(false)` or camera culling-mask flip): simplest, but pops; forum threads on Sims-style interiors converge on splitting walls into upper/lower segments and fading rather than toggling ([Unity discussion](https://discussions.unity.com/t/interior-walls-showing-through-roof-in-third-person/682893)). Acceptable as a greybox placeholder only.
3. **Stencil cutaways**: stencil-buffer masking can carve see-through windows through geometry ([Slide Factory URP stencil article](https://www.theslidefactory.com/post/see-through-objects-with-stencil-buffers-using-unity-urp)) — precise but rigid; better suited to X-ray effects than soft storybook fades.

**Dollhouse detail:** pair the roof fade with the camera move — on entering, blend to an interior vcam that is closer (shorter follow distance), slightly steeper (~45–50°), and confined to the room bounds, with yaw rotation limited (±45° or locked) so the "open dollhouse wall" always faces the camera. This is the Sims Cutaway behavior recreated with two vcams and one shader.

## 5. Occlusion when the player walks behind large plants

Three candidate strategies:

- **Camera collision / de-occlusion (rejected).** Cinemachine's `CinemachineDeoccluder` "preserves line of sight … by moving away from objects that obstruct the view" — every resolution strategy *moves the camera*; there is no built-in fade-the-obstacle mode ([docs](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.cinemachine@3.1/manual/CinemachineDeoccluder.html)). In a composed semi-iso frame, camera dodging reads as glitching. Fine for over-shoulder 3rd-person; wrong tool here.
- **Fade the occluder (primary).** A camera script does `Physics.SphereCastAll` from camera to player each few frames against an `Occluder` layer (hedge banks, large flowers, tree trunks); hit renderers tween their dither-fade alpha down to ~0.25 over ~0.2 s and back up over ~0.4 s when clear. This is the DOS2/BG3-style behavior; Unity implementations are well documented ([Mirza's Realm camera dither fade](http://www.mirzabeig.com/tutorials/camera-dither-fade/), [Medium/DOTS fade-near-camera variant](https://medium.com/@xiangk_81891/unity-dots-2023-fadeout-objects-near-camera-27f5f7f68eb7)).
- **Screen-space see-through circle (secondary, for dense clutter).** Shader draws a soft-edged hole around the player's screen position: Screen Position + aspect-corrected Distance + Smoothstep feeding a Dither→Alpha-Clip, staying in the opaque queue; a camera script converts player position with `WorldToViewportPoint` and `RaycastAll` sets `_CutoutPos`, `_CutoutSize`, `_FalloffSize` on occluding materials. Known limitations: cutout doesn't auto-scale with distance, and thin raycasts can miss adjacent walls — use SphereCast ([Daniel Ilett wall cutout tutorial](https://danielilett.com/2021-03-19-tut5-15-wall-cutout/), [surface-shader gist for built-in RP](https://gist.github.com/kudorgyozo/9e210518ac7cc9938ee96edd15578c7e)).

For Brambly Hedge specifically: whole-object dither fade suits *large discrete* occluders (a hollyhock, a teasel), while the cutout circle suits *continuous* hedge walls where fading the whole 10-meter bank would break the illustration. Ship the fade first; add the cutout circle only if playtests show readability problems in hedge corridors. Never fade small props — the world's charm is its clutter.

## 6. Rotation & input UX

- **Fixed vs stepped vs free.** Tunic ships a completely immutable camera (no rotation, no zoom; lock-on only tilts slightly) and is praised for readability ([Worthplaying](https://worthplaying.com/article/2022/3/16/reviews/131106-pc-review-tunic/), [Tunic wiki controls](https://tunic.fandom.com/wiki/Controls)). Monument Valley snaps world rotation and — key design lesson — only made interaction "decision points" valid when geometry was at a snap position ([Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/making-the-impossible-possible-in-i-monument-valley-i-)). City-builders (Fabledom, Tiny Glade) offer free orbit because building demands it; Fabledom patched in keyboard rotation keybinds and mouse rebinding after launch feedback ([Fabledom patch notes wiki](https://fabledom.fandom.com/wiki/Updates_%26_Patch_notes)).
- **Pivot point matters.** Tiny Glade originally rotated/zoomed around the *cursor*; patch 1.11.2 added the option to rotate and zoom around the *screen center* because cursor-pivot disoriented part of the audience ([patch notes](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/2198150/view/543346166601876515)). For a character game, always pivot around the player/framing target — never the cursor.
- **The 45°-off problem:** with a fixed 45° iso yaw, world axes and screen axes disagree; naive input mapping makes characters drift diagonally. Standard fix: project the camera's forward/right onto the ground plane and build the move vector from those ([Manning, camera-relative movement](https://freecontent.manning.com/programming-camera-relative-movement-controls/), [Micha Davis, isometric movement](https://micha-l-davis.medium.com/isometric-player-movement-in-unity-998d86193b8a)).
- **Rotation-while-moving confusion:** if camera yaw changes while the stick is held, camera-relative "up" changes under the player's thumb mid-walk. Mitigation used across 3rd-person games: **input latching** — while movement input magnitude stays above a threshold, keep translating along the previously computed world direction; recompute the camera basis only when input is released or its direction changes materially. With stepped 45° snaps this matters even more: latch through the snap tween.
- Keep the rotation tween fast and eased (~0.3–0.5 s smoothstep). Slow rotations feel like cutscenes; instant snaps feel like teleports and cause frame-to-frame disorientation.

## 7. Pitfalls

- **Motion sickness is triggered by camera *rotation*, not translation.** Vestibular mismatch comes from the view rotating without the head rotating; guidance across game-camera writeups: camera-shake translation OK, rotation avoid; smooth/damp everything; never animate rotation while the player is actively controlling ([Busseneau](https://nicolas.busseneau.fr/en/blog/2020/09/alleviating-motion-sickness-in-first-person-video-games), [CGCookie](https://cgcookie.com/posts/creating-a-great-game-camera), [MoCap Online cinematics guide](https://mocaponline.com/blogs/mocap-news/cutscene-direction-game-cinematics)). Our arrival reframes should therefore be dominated by dolly/track *translation* with only small pitch/yaw changes, 1.5–2.5 s, EaseInOut, and cancellable by any movement input.
- **Reframing that steals control**: if a blend changes yaw while the player walks, combine with input latching (above) or briefly freeze the camera basis used for movement until the blend ends.
- **Orthographic + rotation = jitter** (see §3) — another reason for perspective.
- **Transparency sorting**: classic alpha-blend fades on overlapping foliage will z-fight and sort wrongly; dithered clip avoids the whole class of bugs ([Ilett](https://danielilett.com/2020-04-19-tut5-5-urp-dither-transparency/)).
- **Trigger-volume camera thrash**: a player standing on a zone boundary can ping-pong two vcams. Use hysteresis (exit collider larger than enter collider) and a minimum-live-time before another blend may start.
- **Blend clipping through geometry**: Brain blends interpolate positions; a blend path may cut through a wall. Keep zone cameras' positions near line-of-sight of each other, or mark the interior/exterior pair to use a Cut or a spline-guided arrival instead.

## 8. Render pipeline decision

Shader Graph works on built-in RP since 2021.2 **but** is officially "for compatibility purposes only — Shader Graph doesn't receive updates for Built-In Render Pipeline support" ([Unity discussion](https://discussions.unity.com/t/does-shader-graph-support-built-in-rendering/700421), [Shader Graph docs](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.shadergraph@14.0/manual/Getting-Started.html)). Everything this camera needs — dither fade, cutout circle, tilt-shift renderer feature, per-object fade with SRP Batcher-friendly property blocks — is URP-first in documentation and community material. In pre-production with near-zero content, the URP migration cost is a day; a year from now it's a month. Recommendation: **URP now**, and evaluate jumping to Unity 6 LTS at the same time (CM 3.1.x behaves identically on both, so camera work is insulated from that decision).

## Implications for Brambly Hedge: The Game

### Recommended camera architecture (Unity + Cinemachine 3.1.x)

**Global setup**
- One `Camera` + `CinemachineBrain`. **Perspective**, FOV **28°** (test band 22–35). Default blend 0.5 s EaseInOut; Custom Blends asset for special pairs.
- Elevation **38°** default (spec band 35–45): low enough for storybook depth and layered backgrounds, high enough for ground readability.
- All gameplay vcams on one Cinemachine Channel; a second channel reserved for photo mode later.

**Player rig (`CM_PlayerFollow`)**
- `CinemachineCamera` + `CinemachinePositionComposer` (framing: player slightly below center, ~0.1 screen-Y bias, dead zone ~0.1×0.1, damping 0.8–1.2 s so the frame breathes) + `CinemachineRotationComposer`.
- **Yaw:** custom `CameraYawController` script — 8 snap steps of 45° (Q/E, bumpers), tween 0.35 s smoothstep. Do not use free orbit at MVP; add a narrow free range (±10° drag) later only if playtests want it.
- **Zoom:** 3 discrete bands (Near/Default/Vista) implemented as follow-distance + FOV pairs lerped over 0.4 s, mouse wheel / triggers stepping between bands. Bands, not analog zoom → every band is an art-directed composition.
- **Movement input:** camera-relative basis from ground-projected camera forward/right, with input latching through yaw tweens.

**Location & interior cameras**
- Per key location: a hand-composed `CinemachineCamera` (fixed or `CinemachineSplineDolly` on a short authored spline for the arrival move), activated by trigger volume (priority bump), blend 1.5–2.5 s EaseInOut, cancelled early by movement input; hysteresis colliders + 2 s minimum live time.
- Per building interior: `CM_Interior_<Name>` — follow distance ~60% of exterior Near band, elevation ~45–50°, yaw clamped to face the dollhouse opening, `CinemachineConfiner3D` to room bounds. Door trigger drives both the vcam swap (0.6–0.8 s blend) and the building shell fade.

**Shader/rendering needs (URP, Shader Graph)**
1. `Lit-DitherFade` — standard lit graph + Dither(8×8)→Alpha Clip driven by `_FadeAlpha`; used by roof shells, upper walls, large occluder plants. MaterialPropertyBlock-driven, fade in 0.2 s / out 0.4 s.
2. `Lit-CutoutCircle` (phase 2) — screen-space soft circle around player screen pos (`_CutoutPos/_CutoutSize/_FalloffSize`, SphereCast-driven) for continuous hedge walls.
3. Tilt-shift Renderer Feature or stock URP DoF — subtle, enabled only at Near band; plus gentle vignette. Off by default at Vista band.
4. `CameraOcclusionFader` C# — SphereCast (radius ~0.3 world units) camera→player on `Occluder` layer, manages fade tweens; `BuildingShellController` — per-building group fade + floor-above hiding, Sims-Cutaway behavior.

**What to copy / what to avoid**
- Copy: Sims 4 Cutaway semantics; Monument Valley "decisions only at snap positions" (interactables stay valid in all 8 yaws — never require a specific camera angle); Tiny Glade's restraint (bounded camera, center-pivot option, soft DoF); Tunic's confidence in composed fixed shots.
- Avoid: Deoccluder camera-push; orthographic projection; alpha-blend fades on foliage; cursor-pivot rotation; free-rotation-by-default; heavy tilt-shift that turns mice into toys; camera yaw changes during active player movement without latching.

### MVP test plan (1–2 weeks, greybox)

1. **Scene:** greybox hedge corridor (~40 m), one two-room cottage with door, one "mill" landmark with arrival trigger, 6 large occluder plants, slope + stream crossing.
2. **Week 1:** Brain + player rig, 45° snap yaw + latched camera-relative movement, 3 zoom bands, trigger-volume vcam for the mill with spline-dolly arrival. *Acceptance:* walk the corridor at all 8 yaws with no control reversals; arrival blend ≤2.5 s, interruptible, never clips geometry.
3. **Week 2:** dither-fade shader + occlusion fader; cottage interior vcam + roof/upper-wall fade with confiner; hysteresis + min-live-time on all triggers; tilt-shift toggle. *Acceptance:* player never fully hidden >0.5 s anywhere on the path; exterior→interior reads as one continuous move (no cut) at 0.6–0.8 s; standing on any trigger boundary produces zero camera ping-pong in 60 s.
4. **Evaluation ritual:** capture 10 random-timing screenshots per playtest; count how many pass "could be a book illustration" by team vote — this operationalizes the pillar and tracks it build over build.
5. **Instrument comfort:** 3+ external testers, ask specifically about the arrival reframe and yaw snapping (the two motion-sickness candidates); test at 60 and 120+ fps since blend feel differs.
