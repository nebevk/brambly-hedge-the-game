# IP Ownership & Licensing: Brambly Hedge

Research date: 2026-07-04. Scope: who controls the Brambly Hedge IP, what has been licensed, how comparable book-IP cozy games got (or avoided) licenses, typical deal structures, and the legal risk of our current working title.

## Key Takeaways

- **The IP is family-owned and actively managed.** Brambly Hedge is controlled by **Four Seasons Licensing and Merchandising Ltd** (UK company no. 08917167), directed by Jill Barklem's children **Peter Barklem** and **Elizabeth (Lizzie) Bryer née Barklem** ([Companies House](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/08917167/officers)). HarperCollins is the publisher, not the rights holder.
- **There is a professional licensing agent:** **Rockpool Licensing** (London, MD **Vickie O'Malley**, vickie@rockpool-licensing.co.uk) has repped Brambly Hedge since ~2019 and brokered the Lupus Films TV option ([License Global](https://www.licenseglobal.com/publishing/rockpool-rep-brambly-hedge), [Licensing.biz](https://licensing.biz/rockpool-licensing-helps-bring-brambly-hedge-book-series-to-screen-in-lupus-films-deal/)). This is who we would pitch.
- **No Brambly Hedge video game exists** — ever, beyond craft CD-ROMs in the 2000s. The category is wide open, but that also means the estate has zero games experience and no precedent deal.
- **The brand is in an active growth phase**: TV adaptation in development at Lupus Films (optioned Feb 2020, still listed "in development" mid-2026), 45th anniversary push in 2025, expanding publishing and merchandise program. The estate is paying attention; unlicensed use will be noticed.
- **Copyright runs until 31 December 2087** (Jill Barklem died 15 Nov 2017; UK/EU life+70). The name is also asserted as a trademark by Four Seasons ([bramblyhedge.com/terms](https://bramblyhedge.com/terms/)). Shipping "Brambly Hedge: The Game" without a license is straightforward infringement, not a grey area.
- **The best-fit precedent is Snufkin: Melody of Moominvalley** — small Norwegian studio Hyper Games pitched family-owned Moomin Characters Oy in 2020, got permission including a *new visual style*, shipped March 2024 to an 83 Metacritic, and got a second Moomin game (April 2026). Family literary estates *do* say yes to small cozy-game studios ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snufkin:_Melody_of_Moominvalley), [moomin.com](https://www.moomin.com/en/blog/snufkin-melody-of-moominvalley/)).
- **The unlicensed path also demonstrably works**: Winter Burrow (Pine Creek Games/Noodlecake, Nov 2025) is an openly Brambly-Hedge-flavoured cozy mouse game — 91% positive on ~1,400 Steam reviews, no legal trouble, because it copies the *vibe* (mice, burrows, knitting, baking) and not the name, characters, or illustrations ([Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3008740/Winter_Burrow/)).
- **Typical deal shape for character-brand licensing**: ~10–15% royalty on net receipts (UK IPO guidance range cited in [GameAnalytics](https://www.gameanalytics.com/blog/part-1-the-pros-and-cons-of-using-a-licensed-ip)), a minimum guarantee/advance, style-guide compliance, and approval gates at concept/art/milestones/marketing. Rights get sliced by platform and territory (Rovio holds *exclusive Moomin mobile* rights separately from Hyper Games' PC/console games — [PocketGamer.biz](https://www.pocketgamer.biz/rovio-exclusive-moomin-mobile-games/)).
- **Blunt bottom line:** do not announce, publish a Steam page, or show public material under the name "Brambly Hedge" without a signed deal. Build the vertical slice under a neutral codename, then pitch Rockpool — and have a legally-distinct rename ready as Plan B from day one.

- **Rights vehicle:** **Four Seasons Licensing and Merchandising Limited**, incorporated 28 Feb 2014, registered at Wadebridge House, 16 Wadebridge Square, Poundbury, Dorchester DT1 3AQ. Active directors: **Peter James Alexander Barklem** (b. 1984) and **Elizabeth Jane Bryer** (b. 1981) — Jill's son and daughter. ## 3. How comparable book-IP cozy games handled licensing

### Snufkin: Melody of Moominvalley (licensed — the model to copy)
- Developer **Hyper Games** (small Oslo studio, Unity), publisher Raw Fury, released 7 March 2024 (PC/Switch), Metacritic 83, DLC late 2024 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snufkin:_Melody_of_Moominvalley)).
- Origin: CEO Are Sundnes conceived it in **2020** while reading a Moomin picture book on parental leave; pitched **Moomin Characters Oy Ltd** — the Jansson **family company** that has managed the IP since the 1950s, with global licensing run through agency **Rights & Brands** ([GamesRadar interview](https://www.gamesradar.com/snufkin-melody-of-moominvalley-reveal-interview/), [rightsandbrands.com](https://rightsandbrands.com/news/snufkin-melody-of-moominvalley-will-be-released-on-on-7-march-2024/)).
- The rights holder allowed a **new visual style** (not a copy of any one Jansson artwork) after "a rigorous process"; per Sundnes the licensor "was enthusiastic about the game throughout and only minor tweaks were needed" ([moomin.com](https://www.moomin.com/en/blog/snufkin-melody-of-moominvalley/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snufkin:_Melody_of_Moominvalley)).
- Design constraint imposed by brand fit: **no violence** — music became the core mechanic. Identical logic to our no-combat pillar.
- Trust compounds: Hyper Games shipped a second Moomin title, **Moomintroll: Winter's Warmth** (27 April 2026, Steam/Switch, publisher Kakehashi Games) ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moomintroll:_Winter%27s_Warmth)).
- Rights slicing: **Rovio separately holds exclusive Moomin *mobile* game rights** ([PocketGamer.biz](https://www.pocketgamer.biz/rovio-exclusive-moomin-mobile-games/)) — proof that PC/console rights can be negotiated as their own carve-out.
- Timeline lesson: idea (2020) → release (2024) = **~4 years including licensing**.

### The Lost Legends of Redwall (licensed — small-studio path, slower)
- Tiny US studio **Soma Games** optioned Redwall video game rights in **2013** via intermediaries (the Miller brothers, who held broader rights from the Redwall Abbey Company), ran a $17,618 Kickstarter in 2014, and only secured an **expanded direct license with the Redwall Abbey Company (the Brian Jacques estate) in late 2017**, shipping the first game in 2018 ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lost_Legends_of_Redwall)). Option-to-ship: **5 years**. Lesson: estates will license to very small teams, but expect a staged option → full license structure and a long runway.

### Miffy (licensed, work-for-hire flavour)
- *Miffy's World* (WiiWare, 2010) was built by Portuguese studio Biodroid "in conjunction with **Mercis bv**", the Bruna family's Amsterdam licensor ([Nintendo Life](https://www.nintendolife.com/news/2010/06/get_cute_and_fluffy_with_miffys_world_on_wiiware)). Small licensed games on beloved picture-book IP have existed for 15+ years; quality bar was historically low.

### The Smurfs (licensed at publisher scale — not our model)
- **Microids** signed with **IMPS** (Véronique Culliford, daughter of Peyo) in 2020, expanded April 2021 to a **four-game, five-year publishing deal** ([Microids](https://www.microids.com/microids-and-imps-the-smurfs-sign-a-major-publishing-deal-regarding-future-smurfs-video-game-adaptations/), [PC Games Insider](https://www.pcgamesinsider.biz/news/72088/microids-signs-publishing-deal-for-four-smurfs-games/)). Family-estate IP again — but licensed to a mid-size publisher with an MG war chest, not to an indie directly.

### Peter Rabbit (cautionary tale on trademarks outliving copyright)
- Beatrix Potter's works are **public domain**, yet **Frederick Warne/Penguin Ventures** still runs an aggressive licensing program (Orchard Toys, Sony Creative Products, WildBrain CPLG) and has litigated trademark rights in book imagery since *Frederick Warne & Co. v. Book Sales Inc.* (S.D.N.Y. 1979), filing trademarks across huge ranges of goods ([Justia](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/district-courts/FSupp/481/1191/2397442/), [The Horn Book](https://www.hbook.com/story/peter-rabbit-and-the-tale-of-a-fierce-bad-publisher), [License Global](https://www.licenseglobal.com/keyword/penguin-ventures)). Notably, **no substantial licensed Peter Rabbit PC/console game exists** — children's-book licensors skew to toys/experiences, so a games pitch is novel to them. Brambly Hedge is in a *stronger* legal position than Warne: full copyright until 2087 **plus** trademarks.

### Unlicensed "inspired-by" (Option B evidence)
- **Winter Burrow** (Pine Creek Games, pub. Noodlecake, released 12 Nov 2025, €18.99): cozy woodland *mouse* survival — burrow restoration, knitting sweaters, baking pies, hand-drawn storybook art. 91% positive (~1,371 reviews), Eurogamer/Polygon raves, Xbox Games Showcase 2024 slot, Game Pass day one ([Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3008740/Winter_Burrow/), [ResetEra](https://www.resetera.com/threads/winter-burrow-announcement-trailer-xbox-games-showcase-2024.895551/)). Players explicitly connect it to Brambly Hedge ([TikTok review](https://www.tiktok.com/@laelindria/video/7407513445026860330)) — and it has faced **zero legal action**, because aesthetic homage without name/characters/compositions is lawful.
- Other proof points: *Littlewood*, *Ghost of a Tale* (mouse adventure openly inspired by Redwall/Mouse Guard), *Bramble: The Mountain King* (even uses "Bramble" in a game title without issue — different word, different trade dress).

## 4. Typical indie licensing deal structure (children's book IP)

- **Royalty:** UK IPO guidance cited for trademark/character licenses is **10–15% of net sales** (can drop to ~5%) ([GameAnalytics](https://www.gameanalytics.com/blog/part-1-the-pros-and-cons-of-using-a-licensed-ip)); in games specifically, licensed-IP royalties commonly land **5–15% of net receipts** depending on brand strength and who funds development ([Legal Moves](https://legalmoveslawfirm.com/video-game-royalties/)). For a mid-tier heritage brand with no games history, expect the agent to open around 10–12% of net with room to negotiate down for a passion-project pitch.
- **Minimum guarantee (MG)/advance:** standard in character licensing; for a niche brand and an indie, MGs can be modest (low five figures spread over the term) but exist to filter unserious licensees. Recoupable against royalties.
- **Approvals:** style-guide compliance plus staged sign-off — concept, character/environment art, key builds (alpha/beta/gold), store page, trailers, and all marketing uses. Moomin Characters ran exactly this and Hyper Games reported it as collaborative, not adversarial ([moomin.com](https://www.moomin.com/en/blog/snufkin-melody-of-moominvalley/)).
- **Scope slicing:** platform (PC/console vs mobile — see Rovio/Moomin), territory, term (typically 3–7 years with sell-off period), sequel/DLC options, merchandising of *game-original* assets usually reserved to licensor.
- **Structure for unproven partners:** an **option agreement** first (cheap, time-boxed, lets you develop a vertical slice legitimately) converting to a full license on greenlight — the Soma/Redwall pattern ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lost_Legends_of_Redwall)).

## 5. Legal risk of "Brambly Hedge: The Game" without a license — be blunt

- **Copyright:** Every character (Wilfred Toadflax, Poppy Eyebright, the Old Oak Palace…), the illustrated cross-section homes, the map of the hedgerow, and the stories are protected in the UK/EU/US until **end of 2087** (life + 70 from Barklem's death in 2017). Recreating recognizable characters, named locations, or Barklem's specific compositions in 3D is derivative-work infringement regardless of what we call the game.
- **Trademark/passing off:** Four Seasons asserts trademark rights in the name ([Terms](https://bramblyhedge.com/terms/)). Using "Brambly Hedge" in the title is use of the mark in trade; even absent a registration in class 9, UK **passing off** would apply because the public would assume official licensing (a famously successful doctrine for character merchandising in the UK). The Warne v. Book Sales line of cases shows children's-book rights holders will litigate over far less ([Justia](https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/district-courts/FSupp/481/1191/2397442/)).
- **Practical enforcement path:** a cease-and-desist to us plus DMCA/notice takedowns to Valve/Nintendo/Microsoft — Steam removes contested pages quickly and a takedown history poisons publisher and platform relationships. For a 1–3 person studio the legal cost asymmetry is total.
- **Probability of detection: high.** The estate is small, online, active (official Instagram/Facebook, D2C shop), agency-managed, and mid-way through a brand relaunch with a TV property in development. A Steam page named "Brambly Hedge" would reach Rockpool within days.
- **Extra wrinkle:** the Lupus Films option means screen/adjacent rights may already be partially encumbered or earmarked; a games license might need to wait for, or be coordinated with, the TV project's look and windowing — or conversely, the estate may *want* a game to land alongside the show.
- **What is NOT protected:** mice in waistcoats, hedgerow/burrow settings, seasonal village life, cross-section interiors as a *general device*, cozy foraging/baking mechanics. Style and genre are free; name, characters, text, and specific artwork are not. Winter Burrow is the live proof of where the line sits.

## 6. Options analysis

### Option A — Pursue the license
- **How:** Legal/register check on "Brambly Hedge" marks → prepare a brand-first pitch (visual target, storybook camera, no-combat community design, respect for Barklem's world; reference Snufkin's critical success and the Moomin estate's positive experience) → approach **Vickie O'Malley at Rockpool** (vickie@rockpool-licensing.co.uk), copying enquiries@bramblyhedge.co.uk. Ask initially for a **12–18 month option** to build a vertical slice, converting to a full PC/console license.
- **Odds:** genuinely plausible but not better than ~30–40%. Pros: no incumbent games licensee, the estate licenses actively, family estates of this exact type (Moomin, Redwall, Smurfs) have all said yes to games, and our design brief is a near-perfect brand match. Cons: 1–3 person team with (presumably) no shipped comparable title; estates in a TV-option phase often freeze adjacent categories; approval culture may be slow; an MG may strain a tiny budget.
- **Timeline:** 3–9 months to a yes/no on an option; 3–5 years pitch-to-ship based on Snufkin (4 yrs) and Redwall (5 yrs).
- **Cost guess:** option fee low four-to-five figures; license at ~10% net royalty with a low-five-figure MG; contract legal fees £5–15k.

### Option B — Rename + original IP ("hedgerow mice" game)
- **What must change:** title (no "Brambly", no "Hedge" pairing that echoes the mark); all character names and designs (no Barklem lookalikes); the map (no Old Oak Palace, Store Stump, dairy, flour mill *ensemble* recreated as-is — individual generic elements are fine); no text or recipes lifted from the books; marketing must never invoke Brambly Hedge ("inspired by classic British picture books" is safe, naming the brand in store copy is not, though press/players will make the connection for free, as with Winter Burrow).
- **What survives untouched:** literally everything in our design pillars — cozy no-combat community sim, seasons, festivals, dollhouse interiors, storybook camera, journal UI. The pitch document loses only the name.
- **Risk:** near-zero legal risk; the real cost is discoverability — but Winter Burrow shows the audience finds "Brambly-Hedge-like" games on aesthetics alone (91% positive, mainstream coverage) ([Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3008740/Winter_Burrow/)).

### Option C — Hybrid: build first, pitch later (recommended)
- Develop the vertical slice now under a **neutral internal codename** with all *named* Brambly Hedge content quarantined to a private, never-published reference layer; build our own character/location names in parallel so the project is Option-B-clean at any moment.
- Pitch Rockpool once the slice demonstrates the storybook camera and one community project end-to-end (strongest possible answer to "can this team ship?"). If yes → license or option; if no or silence after ~2 follow-ups over 3 months → proceed as Option B with zero wasted work.
- **Hard rules for the hybrid window:** no public devlog, Steam page, trailer, or playtest under the Brambly Hedge name; no Barklem artwork in any shared build; keep the pitch deck's use of book imagery clearly marked as rights-holder property used for pitch purposes only.

## Implications for Brambly Hedge: The Game

1. **Rename the working title internally this month.** Even a private repo/Discord named "Brambly Hedge: The Game" becomes discovery-risk the moment anything leaks. Pick a codename now; treat the current name as a pitch aspiration, not an identity.
2. **Adopt Option C formally.** Target: vertical slice by ~Q2 2027, pitch to Rockpool with a 10-page brand-fit deck + playable build; decision gate at most 3 months after first contact, then commit to A or B irrevocably.
3. **Copy the Snufkin pitch, not the Smurfs pitch.** Lead with brand guardianship: no combat, no monetization gimmicks, seasonal faithfulness, "distill the essence" art approach rather than tracing Barklem plates — this exact framing is what won over Moomin Characters ([moomin.com](https://www.moomin.com/en/blog/snufkin-melody-of-moominvalley/)).
4. **Ask for PC/console only, worldwide, 5-year term, with a first option on mobile/sequels.** The Rovio/Moomin split shows platform carve-outs are normal and keep the ask small ([PocketGamer.biz](https://www.pocketgamer.biz/rovio-exclusive-moomin-mobile-games/)).
5. **Budget reality check:** assume ~10% net royalty + low-five-figure MG + £5–15k legal. If that breaks the project's economics, Option B isn't a fallback — it's the plan.
6. **Engineer the codebase for the rename from day one:** all character/location names, book quotes, and story text behind a single localization/data layer; zero Barklem-derived textures or traced silhouettes in the art pipeline. This makes Option B a one-week content swap instead of a rebuild.
7. **Watch the Lupus Films project** ([lupusfilms.com](https://www.lupusfilms.com/films/brambly-hedge)) — a greenlit show would both raise the license price and create the estate's strongest-ever motive to want a game; time the pitch before a broadcaster announcement if possible.
8. **Do a £170 UK IPO trademark search** ([gov.uk](https://www.gov.uk/search-for-trademark)) and a one-hour consult with a UK IP solicitor before the pitch — knowing exactly which classes "Brambly Hedge" is registered in (especially class 9 software / class 41 entertainment) sharpens both the negotiation and the Plan-B boundary line.
