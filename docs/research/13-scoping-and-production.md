# Research 13 — Scoping & Production: What a 1–3 Person Team Can Actually Ship

*Research date: July 2026. Topic: realistic scoping for a handcrafted cozy exploration/life game, comparable-game production data, art pipeline costs, vertical slice and demo strategy, Steam funnel benchmarks, scope triage, and funding options for a (likely) Slovenia-based micro-team.*

## Key Takeaways

- Comparable games split into two cost classes. **"One-verb" cozy exploration games** (A Short Hike, Smushi Come Home, Lil Gator Game, Tiny Glade) ship with 1–3 people in ~1–3 years (≈1–8 person-years). **Life sims with NPC schedules, seasons, and relationships** (Stardew, Ooblets, Wylde Flowers, Cozy Grove) cost ≈8–14+ person-years. Brambly Hedge as currently specced (gathering + cooking + crafting + gardening + fishing + photography + schedules + relationships + 4 seasons + festivals + community projects) is in the **second** class with a **first-class team size**.
- Stardew Valley took one person 4.5 years at ~70 h/week ([Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/the-4-years-of-self-imposed-crunch-that-went-into-i-stardew-valley-i-)). Ooblets took 2 people ~6.5 years (2016 → 1.0 in Sept 2022, [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ooblets)). Wylde Flowers started with ~a dozen people and the team grew ~fourfold over 3 years ([GamesHub](https://www.gameshub.com/news/features/wylde-flowers-studio-drydock-game-development-19489/)). The expensive part is exactly the part Brambly Hedge wants: living NPCs + seasonal content.
- Unpacking's team planned 1.5 years and took roughly double ([GamesRadar](https://www.gamesradar.com/the-making-of-unpacking-developer-witch-beam-on-bringing-the-moving-indie-hit-to-life/)). Plan a 25–30% schedule buffer minimum; assume the 2x rule when unfunded.
- Tiny Glade is the marketing template: 2 devs, ~2 years from announcement to launch, GIF-first marketing → 800k+ wishlists pre-launch, top-10 June 2024 Next Fest demo, 1.37M wishlists and 616k copies ($15) within a month of launch ([GameDiscoverCo](https://newsletter.gamediscover.co/p/how-tiny-glade-built-its-way-to-600k), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiny_Glade)). Its "engine" was one endlessly GIF-able visual system — for Brambly Hedge that is the storybook camera + dollhouse interiors.
- Vertical slice = 3–5 minutes of near-ship-quality gameplay, buildable in ~1–3 months for a small team ([Nineva](https://ninevastudios.com/blog/vertical-slice-game-development-guide), [IndieGames101](https://indiegames101.com/blog/2024-03-04-what-to-expect-from-vs/)). A public **demo** is a different artifact: Gold/Diamond-tier demos hold players for a **median 38–65 minutes** ([HowToMarketAGame benchmark](https://howtomarketagame.com/2022/10/26/what-is-a-good-median-play-time-for-a-demo-benchmark/)).
- Funnel numbers to plan against: ≥2,000 wishlists before entering Next Fest, 7,000 ≈ Popular Upcoming visibility, 30–50k ≈ a $250k+ launch; games entering Next Fest with <1,000 WL gained a median of only 462 ([presskit.gg summarizing Zukowski](https://presskit.gg/field-guides/how-many-wishlists-to-launch)). Median wishlist→week-1-sales conversion is ~0.15x for games with 25k+ WL ([GameDiscoverCo](https://newsletter.gamediscover.co/p/the-state-of-steam-wishlist-conversions)). A game may appear in **only one** Next Fest.
- Stylized 3D outsourcing (2025 rates): props $100–1,500, characters $800–3,500 (to $10k with full rig), environments $1k–10k+, Eastern-EU hourly $30–80 ([RocketBrush](https://rocketbrush.com/blog/game-art-outsourcing-prices-complete-pricing-breakdown), [Pixune](https://pixune.com/blog/game-art-outsourcing-price/), [VSquad](https://vsquad.art/blog/game-art-outsourcing-price-guide-costs-workflow-estimates)). A 500-prop / 12-character handcrafted village outsourced ≈ $100–200k — keep art in-house and cut asset count instead.
- Funding reality 2026: Creative Europe MEDIA's video-game development call gives up to €200k at 60% but **requires a commercially distributed game shipped since Jan 2023** ([Creative Europe Desk IE](https://www.creativeeuropeireland.eu/funding-opportunities/call/video-games-immersive-content)); Epic MegaGrants is alive but now effectively Unreal/UEFN-ecosystem-only, typical $5–75k ([PreMortem](https://premortem.games/2025/02/18/epic-games-introduces-new-cycle-based-submission-process-for-megagrants/)); Slovenia has **no dedicated national game fund** (~400-person industry, [Slovenia Games](https://sloveniagames.com/slovenian-games-industry/)) — the local instrument is the SEF **P2 startup grant (€54,000)** ([startup.si](https://www.startup.si/en-us/news/how-to-get-a-54-000-eur-startup-grant-why-should-you-launch-your-own-ico-project-and-what-does-the-slovenian-startup-ecosystem-have-to-offer-)). Publisher advances are mostly < $50k–$300k with 50–70% dev rev share.
- Recommended structure: a **12-month gated path to a marketable demo** (Plan A), then a hard go/no-go on wishlists + funding before committing to a **24–30 month 1.0** (Plan B). Cut lines are listed at the end.

**The scoping law that falls out of the table:** every game that shipped with ≤3 people in ≤3 years either (a) had essentially **one mechanic** (Tiny Glade, Unpacking, A Short Hike's climb/glide), or (b) was **short** (A Short Hike ~2 h, Smushi ~4 h), or both. Every game with Stardew-class system breadth cost 8–14+ person-years regardless of team size. A 2-person team working 30 months has ~5 person-years — it can afford roughly **half of Stardew's system count**, not all of it plus photography.

## 2. Where the time actually goes

Hard percentage splits are rarely published, but the postmortems converge on consistent qualitative findings:

- **Content, not systems, is the long pole in cozy/life games.** Eastshade's five years were dominated by hand-building an open world's assets, quests and dialogue as (mostly) one person ([postmortem](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/postmortem-eastshade)). Wylde Flowers' 18 hours of dialogue and 350 cutscenes explain its 40+ person team ([GamesHub](https://www.gameshub.com/news/features/wylde-flowers-studio-drydock-game-development-19489/)). Barone spent years redrawing Stardew's art and rebalancing content, not writing the engine ([Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/production/how-i-stardew-valley-i-creator-eric-barone-coped-with-a-four-year-dev-cycle)).
- **The unglamorous tail is huge.** Save systems, options menus, controller glyphs, Steamworks integration, localization plumbing, regression testing. Eastshade's post-launch patching + porting alone cost $60k, and Weinbaum's top process regret was lack of regression/verification testing. One small-game postmortem: core gameplay in 1 week, then 5 more weeks of "art, tuning, testing, debugging, Steam integration" ([Game Developer](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/postmortem-of-my-first-indie-game)) — an 80/20 shape that recurs at every scale.
- **Estimates run ~2x.** Unpacking: planned 18 months, took ~36 ([GamesRadar](https://www.gamesradar.com/the-making-of-unpacking-developer-witch-beam-on-bringing-the-moving-indie-hit-to-life/)). Ooblets: "a few years" became 6.5.
- **What kept A Short Hike on time** ([GDC postmortem](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/finding-smart-shortcuts-in-a-short-hike-postmortem-unlocking-the-vault-4)): an external deadline; a tiered backlog ("core" vs "stretch goals" — the game was shippable without the stretch tier); re-estimating all tasks weekly and daily; art style discovered *from* technical constraints (pixelation hid cheap 3D); aggressive reuse of prior tools.
- **Version control / tooling debt bites micro-teams.** Eastshade lost weeks to Unity Collaborate and a ransomware incident; switched to Git. Set up Git + LFS + automated builds in month 1, not month 18.

Practical planning ratios for a content-driven cozy game (synthesized from the above): **~20–25% systems/engineering, ~45–55% content (art, levels, dialogue, quests, audio), ~25–30% polish/UX/ports/testing/marketing builds.** Whatever the split, schedule content with a measured per-unit cost (see §3) — content scales linearly with scope; systems mostly don't.

## 3. Art pipeline: throughput and cost (stylized 3D, 2025–26 rates)

Outsourcing price benchmarks (use as proxies for in-house time at ~$30–60/h Eastern-EU rates):

- **Props:** simple $100–400, hero pieces $400–1,500 ([RocketBrush](https://rocketbrush.com/blog/game-art-outsourcing-prices-complete-pricing-breakdown), [VSquad](https://vsquad.art/blog/game-art-outsourcing-price-guide-costs-workflow-estimates)) → roughly **2 h–1 day per simple prop, 1–3 days per hero prop** in-house.
- **Characters:** stylized/low-poly $800–3,500; full pipeline (concept→model→texture→rig) up to $10k ([Pixune](https://pixune.com/blog/game-art-outsourcing-price/)) → **1–3 weeks per unique rigged character** in-house; animal characters with clothing (mice in aprons) sit at the upper end because of cloth + quadruped/biped hybrid rigs.
- **Environments:** $1k–10k+ per scene depending on modularity ([RocketBrush environments](https://rocketbrush.com/blog/3d-environment-art-team-hire-cost-pricing-factors-and-how-to-choose-the-right-studio)) → a dollhouse-interior room (Brambly Hedge's signature shot) ≈ 1–2 weeks each including set dressing.
- **Regional hourly rates:** NA/W-EU $60–150+, Eastern Europe $30–80, SE Asia $20–60 ([Juego](https://www.juegostudio.com/blog/outsourcing-game-art-models)).

Back-of-envelope for a handcrafted hedgerow village at announced ambition: ~400–600 props, ~15–25 interiors, ~10–15 rigged characters, 4 seasonal variants of exteriors ≈ **$120–250k outsourced or roughly 2–3 art-person-years in-house** — before VFX, UI illustration, or animation. Levers that cut this 30–60%: one season at demo, seasonal variation via shader/palette + swapped dressing sets rather than remodeling, a shared mouse base-mesh with costume variants (Animal Crossing approach), interiors reusing one modular "burrow kit," and prop families (one bramble kit → 20 placements). Character **animation** is the most under-estimated line item: budget shared rigs + a small shared locomotion/interaction set (~30–40 clips) reused across all NPCs, and buy a retargeting workflow rather than hand-animating per character.

## 4. Vertical slice, demo, and the wishlist funnel

**Vertical slice ≠ demo.** A vertical slice is an internal proof: one polished loop, one environment, real UI/camera/audio, near-final art on key assets, **3–5 minutes** of gameplay; for a small team it should take **~1–3 months** (a solo first slice in 2–8 weeks; if the estimate is longer, the slice is too big) ([Nineva](https://ninevastudios.com/blog/vertical-slice-game-development-guide), [IndieGames101](https://indiegames101.com/blog/2024-03-04-what-to-expect-from-vs/), [Rami Ismail's LTPF](https://ltpf.ramiismail.com/prototypes-and-vertical-slice/)). Its purpose is to run every pipeline once and produce real per-asset cost data for scoping 1.0.

**Public demo benchmarks** (Zukowski's 130-game survey, [howtomarketagame.com](https://howtomarketagame.com/2022/10/26/what-is-a-good-median-play-time-for-a-demo-benchmark/)): median demo playtime tiers — Bronze 7 min, Silver 18, **Gold 38, Diamond 65**; corresponding wishlists earned by the demo: 100 / 500 / 1,200 / 7,000. So the "20–30 min first playable" target is right for a *slice*, but the shipped demo should hold a median player **30–45+ minutes** — for Brambly Hedge that means one full in-game day loop plus a compressed community-project arc, not a walking tour.

**Funnel numbers to build the schedule around:**

- Steam page live **6–12 months before launch**; demo to streamers/curators **2–4 weeks before** any festival ([presskit.gg](https://presskit.gg/field-guides/how-to-build-steam-wishlist), [BigGamesMachine](https://www.biggamesmachine.com/steam-next-fest-marketing-strategies/)).
- Next Fest (Feb/June/Oct): enter with **≥2,000 wishlists** or wait. Games entering with <1,000 WL gained a median of 462; 10k–100k gained a median 6,360; pre-fest WL count predicts fest performance at ρ=0.825 ([presskit.gg / Zukowski Feb-2025 survey](https://presskit.gg/field-guides/how-many-wishlists-to-launch)). **One Next Fest per game, ever** ([Steamworks docs](https://partner.steamgames.com/doc/marketing/upcoming_events/nextfest)) — spend it within ~6 months of launch.
- Visibility thresholds: **7,000 WL** ≈ Popular Upcoming; **30–50k WL** ≈ $250k+ gross launch class ([presskit.gg](https://presskit.gg/field-guides/how-many-wishlists-to-launch)).
- Conversion: median WL→week-1 sales ≈ **0.15x** for 25k+-WL games; Next Fest demo players convert to buyers ~3x better than non-fest wishlists even though in-fest WL conversion dips to 0.752x ([GameDiscoverCo](https://newsletter.gamediscover.co/p/the-state-of-steam-wishlist-conversions), [Alinea Analytics](https://alineaanalytics.substack.com/p/wishlist-to-buyer-conversions-for)).
- The Tiny Glade path — post WIP GIFs of the *visual hook* continuously from announcement; their Dec-2022 trailer + Reddit/Twitter GIFs did most of the work reaching 800k+ pre-launch WL ([GameDiscoverCo](https://newsletter.gamediscover.co/p/how-tiny-glade-built-its-way-to-600k)). Eastshade similarly ran years of screenshot-Saturday + dev streaming ([postmortem](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/postmortem-eastshade)).

## 5. Scope triage: MoSCoW applied to a cozy life game

Evidence for "one great system beats five shallow ones": A Short Hike shipped one traversal verb and won the IGF Grand Prize ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Short_Hike)); Unpacking is literally one mechanic; Tiny Glade shipped a toy so focused the devs refused a roadmap ([GamesRadar](https://www.gamesradar.com/games/city-builder/as-steams-cozy-new-city-builder-tiny-glade-celebrates-500-000-players-devs-explain-why-they-dont-really-want-to-have-a-roadmap/)). The counter-cases: Ooblets (many systems, 2 people, 6.5 years), Wylde Flowers (full breadth, needed 40+ people). The cozy market is also crowded with checklist farming/life sims; differentiation for Brambly Hedge is the **camera/art** and **community-progression**, not system count.

MoSCoW for Brambly Hedge 1.0 (recommendation, justified by §1 math):

- **Must:** storybook camera + dollhouse interiors; exploration; gathering; cooking; **community projects** (the differentiator); NPC daily schedules (simple, deterministic); journal-as-UI; 4 seasons as *presentation + calendar* (palette/dressing/audio swaps, seasonal gates on gathering).
- **Should:** gardening (reuses gathering + time systems); 2 festivals (Spring + Midwinter — festivals are cutscene-plus-minigame content, expensive per minute); light relationship tracking (hearts via journal, no gifting matrix).
- **Could:** fishing (only if it reuses the gathering interaction); photography as a *sketchbook* feature inside the journal (cheap version of the pillar); house customization.
- **Won't (1.0):** romance/marriage, full VO (Eastshade's $10k/25k-word budget VO is the ceiling if any), economy/shop simulation, deep crafting trees, mining/dungeons, multiplayer, procedural anything, more than ~12 scheduled NPCs.

Process rules to copy from A Short Hike: tiered backlog (shippable core + stretch), weekly re-estimation, and one external deadline per quarter (festival submission, Next Fest, publisher milestone).

## 6. Funding options (July 2026, Slovenia-based)

- **Creative Europe MEDIA — Video Games & Immersive Content Development (CREA-MEDIA-DEVVGIM):** up to **€200k lump sum at a 60% funding rate**, for *pre-production/prototype development only* (production phase ineligible). Critical eligibility gate: the applicant company must have **produced/developed a game or immersive work commercially distributed since 1 Jan 2023**. Last deadline was 11 Feb 2026; the call is annual ([Creative Europe Desk Ireland](https://www.creativeeuropeireland.eu/funding-opportunities/call/video-games-immersive-content), [EACEA](https://www.eacea.ec.europa.eu/news-events/news/online-info-session-video-games-and-immersive-content-development-2026-presentation-and-recording-2025-10-29_en)). Slovenia is a MEDIA country — go through CED Slovenia (Motovila) for application support. If no prior commercial release exists, shipping *anything* small commercially (even a premium itch/Steam micro-title) before the 2027 deadline unlocks this — the single largest grant available.
- **Slovenia national:** no dedicated video-game fund; the Slovenian Film Centre funds film/AV only ([film-center.si](https://www.film-center.si/sl/javni-razpisi/)). The industry is ~400 people / ~32 companies with an active association and the Slovenia Games Conference ([sloveniagames.com](https://sloveniagames.com/slovenian-games-industry/)). The practical local instrument is the **Slovene Enterprise Fund P2 startup grant: €54,000 non-refundable** for young innovative companies, plus SK convertible instruments ([startup.si](https://www.startup.si/en-us/news/how-to-get-a-54-000-eur-startup-grant-why-should-you-launch-your-own-ico-project-and-what-does-the-slovenian-startup-ecosystem-have-to-offer-)).
- **Epic MegaGrants:** still running in 2026, now cycle-based (two submission windows/year since Feb 2025), typical awards **$5k–75k** (exceptionally $150k) — but scoped to UE5/UEFN or open-source ecosystem work ([PreMortem](https://premortem.games/2025/02/18/epic-games-introduces-new-cycle-based-submission-process-for-megagrants/), [Unreal](https://www.unrealengine.com/megagrants)). A Unity project should **not** count on it; do not switch engines for a grant.
- **Publishers:** funding typically $100k–$2M at established indie publishers but the realistic band for an unproven micro-team is **< $50k–$300k**; rev shares run 50/50–70/30, and taking an advance costs ~10 points of share on average (57.9% vs 67.9% dev share, GDCo research via [Sanlo](https://www.sanlo.io/resources/what-to-know-about-game-publishing-deals), [firstlook.gg](https://firstlook.gg/blog/indie-game-publishers/)). Cozy-credible publishers to pitch with the demo: Whitethorn, Chucklefish, Raw Fury (published Snufkin), Playtonic Friends (Lil Gator), Mooneye (Smushi), Secret Mode, Future Friends. A licensed-IP game (Brambly Hedge rights sit with the Barklem estate/HarperCollins) makes publisher conversations *easier* if rights are secured in writing first — and impossible if not; resolve the license before any funding pitch.
- **Kickstarter:** 2024 was the platform's best video-game year since 2015 ($26M across 463 projects) and 2025 had 11 campaigns ≥$500k ([ICO Partners](https://medium.com/icopartners/kickstarter-and-video-games-in-2025-90f15c2fd7bd)). Cozy successes: Loftia $1.2M (later +$5M VC, [MassivelyOP](https://massivelyop.com/2025/08/14/kickstarted-cozy-mmo-loftia-secures-5m-in-investor-funding-for-launch-releases-new-trailer/)), Solarpunk $306k vs a $32k goal ([DualShockers](https://www.dualshockers.com/cozy-games-born-from-kickstarter-campaigns/)). Cautionary: PuffPals raised $2.6M and imploded without shipping ([Kotaku](https://kotaku.com/puffpals-fluffnest-cozy-kickstarter-mujin-2000629155)) — the cozy audience is now warier; campaigns work best *after* a playable demo and ~10k+ wishlists, targeting €60–150k. A beloved book IP with 40 years of fans is genuinely strong Kickstarter material — again, contingent on license terms permitting crowdfunding.
- **Realistic budget frame:** Eastshade's $200k covered ~5 frugal years for 1–2 people ([postmortem](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/postmortem-eastshade)). In Slovenia, 2 people × 30 months lean ≈ €140–220k cash need incl. ~€25–50k contracting (music, animation help, trailer, QA/ports). Stack: P2 (€54k) → demo → publisher advance or Kickstarter (€60–250k) → Creative Europe for the *next* project.

## Implications for Brambly Hedge: The Game

The central finding: **the current design doc describes an 8–12 person-year game; the team has ~2.5–5 person-years.** The differentiators (storybook camera, community projects) are cheap-ish; the genre checklist (five gathering-adjacent systems, four festivals, deep relationships) is what kills 2-person teams. Protect the differentiators, gut the checklist, and gate full production behind market proof.

### Plan A — 12 months to a marketable demo (Jul 2026 → Jul 2027)

- **M1–M2 — Look-dev slice.** One hedgerow exterior + one dollhouse interior with roof-fade + one mouse with locomotion/interact; camera fully working (35–45°, limited rotation, smooth zoom, framing volumes). Git+LFS+CI from day 1. **Gate G1 (screenshot test):** post 3–5 GIFs; if none earns meaningful organic traction, iterate art direction *now* — the GIF is the marketing engine (Tiny Glade precedent) and the cheapest thing to fix early.
- **M3–M6 — Vertical slice → internal 20–30 min build.** Summer only. Village square + 3 interiors, 5 NPCs on simple schedules, gather→cook→share loop, one community project compressed to a 3-in-game-day arc (e.g., repair the footbridge: NPCs visibly use it after), journal UI v1, controller + KBM. **Gate G2:** 10–15 external playtesters; median session ≥20 min; players unprompted say "cozy/charming"; and per-asset costs measured in the slice, extrapolated to 1.0 scope, fit Plan B — if not, cut scope *before* production.
- **M4–M5 — Steam page live** (key art, 6–8 screenshots, 45–60 s trailer). Begin weekly GIF cadence. **Gate G3 (M6):** wishlist velocity; target 1,000+ WL before demo release, 2,000+ before Next Fest, else delay the fest (you only get one).
- **M7–M10 — Public demo hardening.** Content for a 30–45 min median playtime (one full day-cycle + project arc + a "golden moment" ending beat — a shared supper scene as the trailer/demo capstone). Save system, options/accessibility, localization-ready text pipeline, performance pass. Demo to cozy streamers/curators 2–4 weeks pre-fest.
- **M10–M12 — Next Fest (June 2027 target; Oct 2027 fallback) + funding push.** Publisher pitch deck built on demo telemetry (median playtime, demo→WL conversion); P2 application; if a prior commercial release exists (or can be shipped), Creative Europe Feb-2027 deadline; Kickstarter only if WL >10k.
