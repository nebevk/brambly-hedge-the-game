# Brambly Hedge Canon Bible — Jill Barklem's Universe Reference

Research compiled 2026-07-04 for the writing team of *Brambly Hedge: The Game*. Primary sources: the official estate site (bramblyhedge.com), Wikipedia, publisher blurbs, and fan/food scholarship. Every non-obvious claim carries an inline source URL. Where sources conflict, the official site is treated as canon and the conflict is flagged.

## Key Takeaways

- **Eight books, one small world.** Four seasonal books (all 1980) + four "expedition/event" books (1983–1994). Total canon is ~8 short picture books — the entire named cast is roughly 20 mice. This is a *tiny, dense, handcrafted* world, exactly matching our "Detail, no procgen" pillar. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brambly_Hedge))
- **Barklem's signature image is the cutaway interior**: tree-trunk homes shown in vertical cross-section with dozens of little rooms, staircases and passages. Our "dollhouse interiors with roof fade" camera is literally canon-native. ([bramblyhedge.com/our-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/our-story/))
- **The community has no money economy and no player-style heroes.** Food goes into the shared Store Stump; big undertakings (Ice Hall, wedding raft, cottage refurbishment, blanket expedition) are done collectively — direct templates for our community-progression system. ([books4yourkids.com](https://www.books4yourkids.com/2018/12/the-brambly-hedge-complete-collection.html?m=1))
- **No combat is canon-accurate**: "There are no unkind characters or predators, apart from a passing reference to weasels." All peril is weather, fog, snow, and getting lost. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brambly_Hedge))
- **Every season has a canonical festival**: spring birthday picnic (Bluebell Bank), Midsummer wedding on a raft, autumn harvest/blackberrying, the Snow Ball in a carved Ice Hall, plus Midwinter's Eve celebrations and Naming Days — a ready-made in-game calendar. ([bramblyhedge.com](https://bramblyhedge.com/))
- **Food and craft culture is exhaustively specified** (primrose puddings, meadowsweet wine, acorn coffee, cowslip wine, chestnut soup; paw-driven looms, water-powered mill and dairy) because Barklem spent years researching real hedgerow botany and rural crafts and tested recipes in her own kitchen. ([bramblyhedge.com/our-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/our-story/))
- **The Sunday Times called the books "the most research-crammed fantasy ever set before small children"** — our art/writing bar should be botanical accuracy, not generic cottagecore. ([bramblyhedge.com/our-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/our-story/))
- **The 1996–2000 stop-motion TV series** (8 × 25 min, HIT Entertainment, narrated by Robert Lindsay) is the best reference for pacing, voice, and gentle scoring. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Enchanted_World_of_Brambly_Hedge))
- **The IP is alive and licensed**: 7M+ copies sold in 20+ languages, estate-run brand, Lupus Films holds a TV option (Feb 2020). Legal status of our game needs resolving early. ([c21media](https://www.c21media.net/news/lupus-film-plants-brambly-hedge/))

## 1. Series Overview & Author

- **Author/illustrator:** Jill Barklem (born Gillian Gaze, 23 May 1951, Epping, Essex — died 15 November 2017, aged 66). A detached retina at 13 pushed her toward art; she studied illustration at St Martin's School of Art, London. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jill_Barklem))
- Brambly Hedge was conceived during her Central Line commutes from Epping, where she "escaped into an inner world, a hedge bank of mice," using the time to research English customs and flora. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jill_Barklem))
- She spent ~5 years on research before publication; each book took ~2 years, single large illustrations up to 3 months. Many trees in the books are drawn from real Epping Forest trees — the City of London later identified a specific ancient tree as the model for Crabapple Cottage. ([bramblyhedge.com/our-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/our-story/), [City of London](https://news.cityoflondon.gov.uk/ancient-tree-which-inspired-brambly-hedge-cottage-discovered-in-epping-forest/))
- Landscape research for later books: **The High Hills** = the Lake District; **Sea Story** = the Norfolk coast. ([bramblyhedge.com/our-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/our-story/))
- First four books published by Collins, autumn 1980, in deliberately **miniature format (17.6 × 14.6 cm)** — the small physical object is part of the brand's intimacy. Sales: 7M+ copies, 13–20+ languages. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brambly_Hedge))
- The estate (her children Lizzie and Pete) runs the brand today via bramblyhedge.com; HarperCollins publishes. Two licensed spin-off storybooks by Alan MacDonald exist: *Wilfred to the Rescue* (2005), *Primrose in Charge* (2006). Audiobooks narrated by John Moffatt. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brambly_Hedge))

## 2. The Eight Books — Plot Summaries

### Spring Story (1980)
Wilfred Toadflax wakes early on his birthday and gets presents, but is disappointed nobody else seems to remember the day. Secretly, Mr Apple has organised a celebration picnic at **Bluebell Bank** and invited every mouse in the hedge. Wilfred lugs a suspiciously heavy hamper along the grassy track — it turns out to hold his birthday surprise, and the whole community feasts in the spring sunshine. ([bramblyhedge.com/spring-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/spring-story/))

### Summer Story (1980) — subtitle in some editions: *Poppy and Dusty's Wedding Day*
A heatwave summer. Poppy Eyebright (the dairy) keeps cool in the mossy shadow of the mill wheel; Dusty Dogwood (the mill) walks the stream banks. They fall in love, announce their engagement to nobody's surprise, and marry on **Midsummer's Day on a raft moored on the stream**. Mishaps: Dusty spills flour over himself; the wedding raft breaks its ropes mid-ceremony and drifts into the reeds. ([Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/421572.Summer_Story), [bramblyhedge.com/dusty-dogwood](https://bramblyhedge.com/dusty-dogwood/))

### Autumn Story (1980) — subtitle: *Primrose Meets the Harvest Mice*
Harvest: all mice race to gather seeds, berries and roots into the Store Stump before rain. Primrose, Lord Woodmouse's daughter, daydreams her way across the cornfield into the **Chestnut Woods** and gets lost — picking wildflowers, meeting harvest mice and exploring a vole colony — while the adults mount an increasingly frantic search. Night falls, wind and rain rise; she is found and brought safely home. ([Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/421625.Autumn_Story), [AbeBooks edition subtitle](https://www.abebooks.co.uk/9780006640691/Autumn-Story-Primrose-Meets-Harvest-0006640699/plp))

### Winter Story (1980)
The deepest snow in years buries every door and window; mice dig tunnels to reach each other. "There's enough for a Snow Ball!" — the community builds an **Ice Hall** "in the time-honoured way" (carved snow-block architecture), and the whole hedge celebrates with a ball; the children are allowed to stay up. ([bramblyhedge.com/winter-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/winter-story/))

### The Secret Staircase (1983)
**Midwinter's Eve.** The hedge prepares the traditional celebrations at Old Oak Palace (blazing fire, grand entertainment). Primrose and Wilfred, due to recite a poem, go up to the palace attics to rehearse, find a small key in an old dresser, then a hidden door and a **dusty winding staircase** leading to a magnificent forgotten apartment at the top of the Old Oak — trunks of old-fashioned finery included. They descend in splendid antique costume for their recital, keep the rooms as their secret, and fall asleep dreaming of games in their hidden house. ([bramblyhedge.com/the-secret-staircase](https://bramblyhedge.com/the-secret-staircase/), [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/421584.The_Secret_Staircase))

### The High Hills (1986)
Late autumn. The voles of the High Hills need new blankets — moths have eaten theirs — so weavers **Flax and Lily** work overtime and **Mr Apple** must deliver the blankets before winter. Wilfred, deep in a book about the explorer **Sir Hogweed Horehound** and his discovery of gold in the high hills, begs to come along to prospect. He scrambles up a rock face and can't get down; mist engulfs him and Mr Apple, who spend a cold night on the mountain (Wilfred's ever-packed explorer's backpack proves its worth) before finding their way home — Wilfred returning with a "treasure" after all. ([Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/421577.The_High_Hills), [bramblyhedge.com/the-high-hills](https://bramblyhedge.com/the-high-hills/), [bramblyhedge.com/mr-toadflax-mrs-toadflax](https://bramblyhedge.com/mr-toadflax-mrs-toadflax/))

### Sea Story (1990) — subtitle: *Primrose and Wilfred Sail to Sandy Bay*
The hedge's salt is running low. Dusty, Poppy, Primrose and Wilfred sail Dusty's boat, the **Periwinkle**, downstream all the way to the sea to fetch salt from the sea mice. They are hosted by **Purslane and Thrift Saltapple** and family at **Sandy Bay**, gather salt at **Seagull Rock**, weather a storm ("it wasn't all plain sailing"), and return home with the precious cargo. ([bramblyhedge.com/sea-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/sea-story/), [Amazon edition subtitle](https://www.amazon.com/Sea-Story-Primerose-Wilfred-Brambly/dp/0006645984))

### Poppy's Babies (1994)
Early summer. Poppy and Dusty's three babies — **Rose, Buttercup and Pipkin** — are crying in the cramped, clattering, flour-dusted mill. The community secretly refurbishes a new home for the family (**Mayblossom Cottage**, near the Dairy), with even Wilfred keeping the secret, and reveals it as a surprise on the babies' **Naming Day**, where Old Vole gives the blessing. ([bramblyhedge.com](https://bramblyhedge.com/), [bramblyhedge.com/poppy-eyebright](https://bramblyhedge.com/poppy-eyebright/), [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/529154.Poppy_s_Babies))

## 4. Locations & Canonical Geography

The hedge is a real English hedgerow bordering a **cornfield**, with a **stream** running past; endpaper maps in each book chart the homes along the hedge, and the later books extend the map upland (High Hills) and downstream (to the sea). ([books4yourkids.com](https://www.books4yourkids.com/2018/12/the-brambly-hedge-complete-collection.html?m=1))

- **Old Oak Palace** — Lord & Lady Woodmouse's seat and the community's ceremonial heart: enormous, full of noble rooms, ancient furniture, carvings, attics, secret passages, and (post-1983) the forgotten apartment atop the secret staircase. Venue for the Midwinter celebrations. ([bramblyhedge.com](https://bramblyhedge.com/lord-lady-woodmouse/))
- **The Store Stump** — communal food store run by Mr Apple: a central hall leading off to many passages, staircases and storerooms stocked with pickles, jams, honey and nuts; Mr Apple "ensures that there is always something good to eat on the shelves." The logistical heart of the hedge and of every festival. ([bramblyhedge.com](https://bramblyhedge.com/mr-mrs-apple/), [books4yourkids.com](https://www.books4yourkids.com/2018/12/the-brambly-hedge-complete-collection.html?m=1))
- **Crabapple Cottage** — the Apples' home in a crabapple tree; modelled on a real Epping Forest tree. ([City of London](https://news.cityoflondon.gov.uk/ancient-tree-which-inspired-brambly-hedge-cottage-discovered-in-epping-forest/))
- **The Hornbeam Tree** — the Toadflax family home, next door to the Apples. ([bramblyhedge.com](https://bramblyhedge.com/mr-toadflax-mrs-toadflax/))
- **The Flour Mill** — Dusty's water-powered mill on the stream; noisy, clattering, flour-dusted; the Dogwood family's first home. ([bramblyhedge.com](https://bramblyhedge.com/dusty-dogwood/))
- **The Dairy** — Poppy's domain (before her, Old Mrs Eyebright's): butter and cheese, heavy pans, scrubbed stone slabs; by the stream near the mill wheel. ([bramblyhedge.com](https://bramblyhedge.com/mrs-eyebright/))
- **Elderberry Lodge** — Basil's home and cellar of wines, syrups and cordials. ([bramblyhedge.com](https://bramblyhedge.com/basil-brightberry/))
- **Mayblossom Cottage** — the refurbished cottage near the Dairy given to Poppy and Dusty's family in *Poppy's Babies*. ([bramblyhedge.com](https://bramblyhedge.com/poppy-eyebright/))
- **Old Vole's hole** and (per secondary sources) **Hawthorn-bank cottage of Old Mrs Eyebright** — minor elder dwellings along the hedge. (Naming varies by source; verify against book endpapers before final naming.)
- **Bluebell Bank** — spring picnic meadow (*Spring Story*). ([bramblyhedge.com/spring-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/spring-story/))
- **The cornfield & Chestnut Woods** — harvest ground and the wood where Primrose gets lost (*Autumn Story*). ([Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/421625.Autumn_Story))
- **The High Hills** — misty upland beyond the hedge; vole settlements, rock faces, legendary gold (Lake District reference landscape). ([bramblyhedge.com/our-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/our-story/))
- **The stream → the sea: Sandy Bay & Seagull Rock** — the Periwinkle's voyage route; sea-mouse settlement and salt-gathering rocks (Norfolk coast reference landscape). ([bramblyhedge.com/our-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/our-story/))

## 5. Festivals, Ceremonies & Seasonal Events

- **Birthdays** — community-scale: Wilfred's birthday triggers an all-hedge surprise picnic. ([bramblyhedge.com/spring-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/spring-story/))
- **Midsummer's Day wedding** — raft on the stream, flower-decked; feast of cold watercress soup, fresh dandelion salad, honey creams, syllabubs, meringues and wild strawberries, with meadowsweet or elderflower wine cooled in the stream. Weddings are officiated by Lord Woodmouse with Old Vole. ([jamarattigan.com](https://jamarattigan.com/2020/11/10/celebrating-40-years-of-brambly-hedge-with-apple-cake/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brambly_Hedge))
- **Harvest & blackberrying** — the whole hedge gathers seeds, berries and roots into the Store Stump before the autumn rains; blackberry picking for the winter store. ([Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/421625.Autumn_Story))
- **The Snow Ball** — held only when snow is deep enough; an **Ice Hall** is built "in the time-honoured way" and the hedge dances the night away. ([bramblyhedge.com/winter-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/winter-story/))
- **Midwinter's Eve** — the winter-solstice festival: a **midwinter log hauled along the frosty hedgerow** as centrepiece, blazing fire, palace decorated, feasting, and a "grand entertainment" of recitals and performances by the mice themselves. ([bramblyhedge.com/winter-2023](https://bramblyhedge.com/winter-2023/), [bramblyhedge.com/the-secret-staircase](https://bramblyhedge.com/the-secret-staircase/))
- **Naming Day** — christening-equivalent for new babies; blessing given by Old Vole; occasion for community gifts (a whole house, in *Poppy's Babies*). ([Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/529154.Poppy_s_Babies))
- **Engagements** — publicly announced and celebrated (*Summer Story*).

## 6. Food, Drink & Craft Culture

**Cooking/feasting (documented dishes):** primrose puddings, freshly baked buns, cheeses, pies, honey, bramble brandy (spring picnic); Mrs Apple's seed cake with hot acorn coffee; cold watercress soup, dandelion salad, honey creams, syllabubs, meringues, wild strawberries (wedding); hot chestnut soup (first snow); rhubarb crumble; clover bread and blackberry buns (Dusty); rosehip jam (Mrs Apple). ([jamarattigan.com](https://jamarattigan.com/2020/11/10/celebrating-40-years-of-brambly-hedge-with-apple-cake/), [therereaderskitchen.com](https://therereaderskitchen.com/tag/brambly-hedge/))

**Drinks (Basil's seasonal cellar):** cowslip wine (spring); elderflower, wild raspberry, sweet briar and clover cordials (summer); blackberry, elderberry and crabapple syrups (autumn); celebration punches; meadowsweet and elderflower wine. ([bramblyhedge.com](https://bramblyhedge.com/basil-brightberry/))

**Production & craft:** water-powered flour mill; dairy (butter, cheese); **paw-driven looms** for spinning and weaving cloth and blankets; seasonal preserving (jams, pickles, syrups) stockpiled in the Store Stump; boatbuilding (the Periwinkle) and raft-building; snow-block architecture (Ice Hall); house refurbishment as a community craft project; patchwork quilts and cosy furnished burrows throughout the illustrations. ([bramblyhedge.com/our-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/our-story/), [lizziebickerstaff.substack.com](https://lizziebickerstaff.substack.com/p/the-eco-magic-of-brambly-hedge))

**Knowledge culture:** recipes and daily records are handed down matrilineally — Mrs Apple's journal + her mother's/grandmother's/great-grandmother's diaries. ([bramblyhedge.com](https://bramblyhedge.com/mr-mrs-apple/))

## 8. Aesthetic & Values

- **Victorian-pastoral England in miniature**: mice in period rural dress, dressers, ranges, pantries, oil lamps and candlelight, formal manners at the palace alongside working-class practicality at the mill — merchandised since the 1980s on Royal Doulton china, pewter miniatures (Hantel), enamels (Crummles) and Border Fine Arts figurines. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brambly_Hedge))
- **Ecological interdependence**: the mice's "joys and tribulations often pivot first on the ecological and second on the personal" — plots are driven by seasons, weather, food supply, not villains. ([lizziebickerstaff.substack.com](https://lizziebickerstaff.substack.com/p/the-eco-magic-of-brambly-hedge))
- **Self-sufficiency + mutual aid**: everyone has a trade; surplus is shared through the Store Stump; the strong provision the weak (blankets for the voles); big life events are community productions. ([bramblyhedge.com/our-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/our-story/))
- **Gentle stakes, real warmth**: the worst that happens is being lost, cold, or fogged in; resolution is always search, shelter, and coming home to food and firelight.
- **Density of detail as wonder**: the signature reader experience is poring over cutaway interiors and hedgerow botany — "the most research-crammed fantasy ever set before small children." ([bramblyhedge.com/our-story](https://bramblyhedge.com/our-story/))
- **Class without conflict**: an aristocracy (Woodmouse) exists but is purely ceremonial-benevolent; a lord's daughter and a miller's nephew-figure are best friends; commoner Wilfred and Lady Primrose share every adventure.

## Implications for Brambly Hedge: The Game

1. **Make the cutaway the hero shot.** Barklem's cross-section interiors are the IP's most recognisable asset and validate our Storybook Isometric Camera exactly. Build every major interior (Store Stump, Old Oak Palace, Mill) as a true vertical dollhouse — 3–6 floors of small rooms connected by stairs and passages — and let roof/wall fade reveal it Sims-4-style. Target the Palace as the flagship: it canonically contains secret passages and a hidden apartment, i.e. built-in exploration gameplay (*The Secret Staircase*).
